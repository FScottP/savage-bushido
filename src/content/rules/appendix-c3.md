Shinto miracles arise from relationship with the Kami — the spirits of the natural world, the ancestors, and the sacred places of Nippon. The Shinto Gakusho does not command the Kami; they petition them, maintain the relationships through ritual purity, and receive power as an expression of the Kami's favor.

All Shinto powers use the Shinten skill (Spirit). Full PP recovery requires proximity to a shrine or sacred site. Kegare accumulates through contact with death, blood, illness, and ritual transgression — see Chapter Eight for full rules.

## Core Principles of Shinto Power

**Relationship over command:** Shinto miracles work through relationship. A Shinto Gakusho who has maintained ritual purity, honored the appropriate Kami through ceremony, and acted in accordance with Shinto values finds their powers responsive and powerful. A Gakusho who has accumulated Kegare and neglected ceremony finds their connection weak.

**Place matters:** Many Shinto powers are enhanced at shrines and sacred sites. A Gakusho performing *exorcism* at the Kami's own shrine operates with the Kami's full backing. The same power in a battlefield covered with corpses operates against significant spiritual resistance.

**Kegare and access:** At Kegare 3+, Sacred Texts are blocked and Notice rolls to sense Kami become unreliable. At Kegare 5+, all Shinten rolls suffer −4, PP recovery is halved, and the Gakusho cannot sense spiritual presences at all — the Kami withdraw.

**The spirit world:** Shinto Gakusho work with the spirit world as it exists in the physical world — the Kami of this river, the spirit of that mountain, the ancestor of this family. Their powers reflect this intimate, local knowledge of the spiritual landscape.

## Shinto Power Descriptions

### Ward Boundary
*Novice, 3 PP, Special, 1 hour*

New Power. The caster walks the perimeter of an area while reciting a norito, laying a sacred boundary. The area may be up to Smarts × 10 yards in diameter. Requires 5 minutes of uninterrupted walking and recitation — not usable in combat as an immediate action.

Within the Ward Boundary for the duration:
- Kegare does not accumulate from mundane sources (death contact, blood, etc.) for any character inside.
- Supernatural beings who are spiritually impure (Oni, Araburu-kami, Yurei with unresolved attachment) must make a Spirit roll (TN 4) to enter. Failure means they cannot enter this round and must try again next round.
- The caster and allies within the boundary gain +1 to all Spirit rolls.

The boundary is invisible to mundane perception but clearly visible to those with Kami Sense or Spirit Sight. A small shimenawa (sacred rope) may be used to mark the boundary physically — common practice for permanent shrine boundaries.

### Speak with Kami
*Novice, 2 PP, Self, 5 minutes*

New Power. Enables direct verbal communication with a Kami present in the current location. The Kami must be present — this power does not summon or call a Kami, it opens communication with one that is already there.

**What Kami know:** A Kami of a specific location knows everything that has occurred within its domain. The river Kami knows what has been thrown into the river, who has crossed it, and when. The mountain Kami knows the paths taken by travelers on its slopes. The household Kami knows the family's history. They may choose not to share what they know — Kami have their own relationships with the truth.

**Communication:** The Kami communicates through impressions, images, and feelings rather than spoken language — the Shinten roll represents the Gakusho's ability to receive and interpret these communications accurately. A success provides useful information. A raise provides specific, detailed information.

**Kegare complication:** At Kegare 2+, the Kami may be reluctant to speak clearly — the spiritual impurity creates static in the communication. The caster makes the Shinten roll at −2. At Kegare 4+, the Kami refuses to speak at all.

### Exorcism Norito
*Seasoned, 3 PP, Smarts", Special*

New Power. The Shinto equivalent of Buddhist *exorcism* — driving supernatural beings from a location through the invocation of Kami authority.

**Against Oni:** Opposed Shinten vs. Spirit. With success, the Oni is pushed back 3" and cannot advance toward the caster for 3 rounds. With a raise, the Oni is driven entirely from the location for the remainder of the scene.

**Against Yurei:** Opposed Shinten vs. Spirit. With success, the Yurei is driven from the location for 24 hours. With a raise, for one week. The Yurei returns when the duration expires unless its Unfinished Business has been resolved.

**Against Araburu-kami:** +2 Spirit to resist — the Araburu-kami belongs in the mortal world and resists exile. Success drives it back 5" and suppresses its domain powers for 3 rounds. A raise suppresses for the scene.

**At a shrine:** If the exorcism is performed within a recognized shrine boundary or Ward Boundary, the caster gains +2 to the Shinten roll. The Kami of the location adds its authority to the exorcism.

**Kegare complication:** The Exorcism Norito requires a minimum of ritual purity. At Kegare 3+, the power cannot be cast. The Kami will not lend authority to a spiritually impure supplicant.

### Misogi Ritual
*Seasoned, 2 PP, Touch, Instant*

New Power. Purification through water — the fundamental Shinto act of cleansing. The target must be in or near running water (a river, stream, or cascade — still water is insufficient). The caster recites the purification norito while the target stands in the water.

**Effect:** Removes 2 Kegare from the target (1 Kegare with a standard success; 2 Kegare with a raise). May target the caster themselves.

**Extended Misogi:** If the ritual is performed for a full 30 minutes (not usable in combat), it removes all Kegare from one target without a Shinten roll — it succeeds automatically.

**Sacred water:** At a shrine with a dedicated misogi basin or waterfall, the 30-minute ritual removes all Kegare from up to five targets simultaneously.

**The Kegare limit:** Misogi cannot remove Kegare that accumulated from deliberate, serious taboo violations (killing an innocent, desecrating a shrine, breaking a solemn oath). These require the Great Purification ritual or formal shrine ceremony.

### Storm Ward
*Seasoned, 3 PP, Special, 1 hour*

New Power. The caster establishes a ritual boundary that affects weather within Smarts × 20 yards. Must be performed outdoors.

Choose one effect at casting:
- **Calm:** Reduces wind to a gentle breeze, stops precipitation, prevents lightning within the area. Existing storm conditions outside the boundary continue — the ward creates a pocket of calm.
- **Redirect:** Diverts wind around the boundary, protecting structures and ships within from storm damage. Ranged attacks within the ward are not affected by wind penalties.
- **Summon rain:** Creates steady rainfall within the boundary. Useful for agricultural blessing or fire suppression. The rain is gentle — it does not create tactical penalties but extinguishes small fires within 3 rounds.

Storm Ward cannot create a storm — only moderate or redirect one that already exists (for Calm and Redirect) or create gentle rain (for rain summoning). For storm creation, see Taming the Storm in Appendix C-1.

### Kami's Wrath
*Seasoned, 4 PP, Smarts", Instant*

New Power. The caster calls on the Kami's elemental power directly. The specific element depends on the dominant Kami of the current location — fire near a volcanic mountain, water near a river, earth in farmland, wind in open country.

This power functions as a *bolt* or *blast* depending on the PP spent:
- **4 PP (bolt):** Single target, 2d6+2 damage, AP 2. The element's type (fire, water, earth, wind) applies as a trapping.
- **6 PP (blast):** Medium Blast Template, 2d6 damage, element trapping.

**Location dependence:** If no dominant Kami is present (in a city far from natural features, in a dungeon), the power costs +2 PP as the caster must work without local spiritual support.

**Kegare:** Using Kami's Wrath accumulates +1 Kegare regardless of target — the Kami's power is being directed toward violence, which is spiritually costly even when justified.

### Ancestor's Counsel
*Seasoned, 3 PP, Self, 10 minutes*

Modified Speak with Dead. The caster communicates with the spirit of a specific deceased ancestor or former leader whose Segaki funeral rites have been properly performed.

**Requirements:** The caster must know the ancestor's name and lineage. A personal possession of the ancestor or proximity to their grave aids the connection (+1 to Shinten roll). The Segaki rites must have been completed — spirits for whom proper funeral rites have not been performed are Yurei, not accessible ancestors.

**What ancestors know:** Ancestors know what they knew in life plus what they have observed from the spirit realm since death. They may have perspective on situations that mortals cannot see, but their information is not omniscient — they see through a spiritual lens that may miss mundane details.

**The ancestor's will:** Ancestors cannot be compelled. They choose what to share. An ancestor who disapproves of the current family head may be unhelpful or actively discouraging. An ancestor who was wise in life may give cryptic rather than direct guidance.

**Kegare:** Ancestor's Counsel accumulates +1 Kegare regardless of the ancestor's nature — contact with the dead, even respectfully, disrupts ritual purity.

### Holy Ground
*Veteran, 5 PP, Special, 1 hour*

New Power. Creates a temporary sacred zone that functions as a minor shrine for the duration. The area may be up to Smarts × 5 yards in diameter. Requires 10 minutes of ceremony to establish.

Within Holy Ground for the duration:
- All Shinto Gakusho within the area recover PP at enhanced rate (as if at a shrine).
- Kegare does not accumulate from any source for characters within the area.
- Oni cannot enter (automatic barrier, no roll required).
- Yurei are pushed to the edge of the area automatically and cannot re-enter while the Holy Ground is active.
- All characters within gain +2 to Spirit rolls against Fear from supernatural sources.

Holy Ground requires a Shinten roll (TN 4) to establish. On a critical failure, the ceremony fails and the PP are lost — the location is spiritually resistant (possibly because something wrong is present that blocks consecration — a clue for the GM).

### Binding of the Kami
*Veteran, 4 PP, Smarts", Special*

New Power. Compels a supernatural being of equal or lesser Spirit to obey a single command through Kami authority.

**Targeting:** Cannot target Oni — their nature resists Kami authority entirely. Cannot target beings with Spirit higher than the caster's Shinten die type.

**Mechanism:** Opposed Shinten vs. Spirit. With success, the being is compelled to perform one specific, finite service within its nature and capability. The service must be clearly defined at the time of casting.

**Completion and limits:** As with Buddhist Divine Mandate — the being performs the service when released but the power cannot override fundamental spiritual reality. A Yurei cannot be commanded to leave a location permanently if Unfinished Business holds it there.

**Kami relationships:** Using Binding against a Kami is considered spiritually rude — the Gakusho is compelling rather than petitioning. Future encounters with that Kami begin at Unfriendly Disposition. Repeated binding of the same Kami eventually results in the Kami withdrawing its favor from the Gakusho entirely — they become spiritually deaf to that Kami's presence.

### Great Purification (Oharae)
*Veteran, 5 PP, Special, Instant*

New Power. The Great Purification — the most significant Shinto cleansing ritual. The Oharae clears all Kegare from the caster and up to five designated targets simultaneously.

**Requirements:** Must be performed at a shrine or within an active Ward Boundary or Holy Ground. Requires 1 hour of ceremony. Cannot be rushed — there is no combat version. Cannot clear Kegare that accumulated from the most serious taboo violations (killing an innocent in cold blood, desecrating a Kami's sacred space, betraying an oath sworn at a shrine) — these require GM-determined special atonement.

**The ceremony:** The caster leads the targets in reciting the Oharae no Kotoba (the Great Purification norito). At the conclusion, the Kami of the location witnesses the purification. Make a Shinten roll (TN 4). With success, all Kegare is removed from all targets. With a critical failure, the ritual fails — the Kami is not satisfied. Failing characters may attempt the ritual again after seven days.

**Kegare note:** Performing the Great Purification does not itself accumulate Kegare — it is one of the few extended ceremonies that is spiritually clean.

### Divine Mandate
*Veteran, 4 PP, Smarts", Special*

New Power. Invokes a Kami's authority to compel a supernatural being. Mechanically similar to Buddhist Divine Mandate but with important differences in application and consequence.

**Targeting:** May target Yurei, Kitsune, Tanuki, and minor Kami of lesser Spirit than the caster. Cannot target Oni (wrong spiritual authority) or major Kami of the high pantheon.

**Mechanism:** Opposed Shinten vs. Spirit. With success, the being is compelled to perform one clearly defined service within its nature.

**The difference from Buddhist version:** Shinto Divine Mandate is grounded in Kami hierarchy — the caster is invoking the authority of higher Kami over lower spiritual beings. This means it is less spiritually costly when used appropriately (compelling a Yurei through the authority of an ancestor Kami is considered proper) but more costly when misused (compelling a Kami of equal or near-equal standing through the authority of another Kami creates spiritual conflict — +2 Kegare immediately).

**The target's memory:** Unlike the Buddhist version, the target of Shinto Divine Mandate retains full memory of being compelled. This is by design — the Kami hierarchy is transparent, not hidden. The being knows they were commanded and by whose authority.

### Consecrate Ground
*Heroic, 8 PP, Special, 1 month*

New Power. Creates permanent (one month) sacred ground — a functional minor shrine anywhere the caster performs the ceremony.

**Requirements:** One hour of ceremony. A physical marker must be placed — a shimenawa (sacred rope), a small torii gate, or at minimum a carved wooden post with the appropriate norito inscribed. Shinten roll (TN 6).

**Effects:** As Holy Ground (Veteran power) but lasting one month. Additionally:
- Shinto Gakusho within the area gain full enhanced PP recovery permanently for the duration (not just for one hour).
- Minor Kami of the area are summoned and become permanent residents of the consecrated space — they provide passive protection and alert the caster (through their Kami Sense) to any intrusion.

**Renewal:** Can be renewed each month through a 30-minute ceremony (no Shinten roll required for renewal of an existing consecration). May be made permanent through a Legendary-level campaign action — finding, commissioning, or crafting a proper shrine structure and performing the full inaugural ceremony.

### Voice of the Kami
*Heroic, 6 PP, Special, Special*

New Power. Once per session. A Kami speaks directly through the caster — the caster's voice changes, their eyes may go blank, and the Kami's message is delivered.

**The Kami's choice:** The caster does not control what the Kami says. They invite the Kami to speak and become a vessel. The GM determines the Kami's message based on what the Kami would actually want to communicate. The caster may specify which Kami they are inviting — but the Kami decides whether to accept the invitation.

**The message:** The Kami's communication is always truthful from the Kami's perspective. It may be cryptic, may focus on things the Kami cares about that the party does not, or may be alarmingly direct. The Kami is not trying to be helpful in a human sense — they are expressing their own understanding of the situation.

**Mechanical effect:** All characters who hear the Kami's voice make a Spirit roll (TN 6). Failure means they cannot take any action contrary to the Kami's expressed will for the remainder of the scene. Success means they receive the message and may act as they choose — but they know what the Kami wants.

**The caster's experience:** After Voice of the Kami, the caster has no memory of what was said — the Kami's speech bypassed their conscious mind. They learn what was communicated only from others who heard it.

**Kegare:** Voice of the Kami accumulates no Kegare — it is the purest possible Shinto act.

### Kami Avatar
*Legendary, 10 PP, Self, Spirit rounds*

New Power. The caster becomes a temporary vessel for a Kami — a full spiritual possession by invitation, rather than the partial communication of Voice of the Kami.

**Requirements:** The caster must have a Group Rank of 4+ in a Shinto shrine organization, must have zero Kegare at the time of casting, and must be at a recognized shrine or consecrated ground. The Kami must be one with whom the caster has an established relationship.

**The transformation:** For Spirit rounds, the Kami inhabits the caster's body. During this time:
- The caster's physical Attributes each increase by one die type.
- The caster gains access to up to 5 powers associated with the Kami's domain at Legendary effectiveness — determined by the GM based on the Kami's nature.
- The caster is immune to all Kegare accumulation.
- The caster has no control over their actions — the Kami acts through them. The player describes their character's behavior in broad terms; the GM adjudicates what the Kami actually does.

**After the Avatar:** When the power ends, the caster is Exhausted (maximum Fatigue) for 24 hours. They retain no memory of the Avatar period — they know time passed and something significant happened. They gain +2 On from the event (the world witnessed a genuine divine manifestation).

**The Kami's agenda:** The Kami uses the Avatar to accomplish something it cares about — which may or may not align with the party's immediate goals. A water Kami might use the Avatar to purify a corrupted river regardless of whether the party needed that purification right now. The GM should use Kami Avatar as a narrative event with significant consequences, not merely a buff.

## Kamimusha — Warrior Priests of the Kami

Kamimusha are senior members of the Shinto Shrine organization who have taken on the warrior-protection role. They differ from Sohei in serving specific local Kami rather than Buddhist institutional authority.

**Power access:** Kamimusha have full access to the Shinto power list through their Shinten skill. Their starting powers typically emphasize protection and exorcism over healing — they are defenders, not healers.

**Recommended Kamimusha starting powers:** *protection* (Ward Boundary — keeping the shrine space sacred), *exorcism* (driving off supernatural threats), *healing touch* (sustaining the community they protect).

**Kamimusha and Kegare:** Kamimusha accumulate Kegare at half rate when engaged in direct defense of the shrine, its sacred spaces, and the community the Kami protects. Offensive operations outside this mandate accumulate Kegare at normal rate.

**The naginata and bow:** Traditional Kamimusha weapons are the naginata (for close defense) and the yumi great bow (for distance). Many Kamimusha take Naginatajutsu and Kyujutsu as their Specialist Bugei Edges. The combination of spiritual power and martial training makes experienced Kamimusha among the most formidable defenders in Nippon.
