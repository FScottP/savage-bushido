Nippon is alive with spiritual force. The mountains breathe. Rivers have personalities. The dead do not always stay where they are put. For most people, this spiritual reality is background — acknowledged, propitiated, occasionally feared, rarely engaged directly. For the Shugenja, the Gakusho, and the Sohei, the spiritual world is their primary workplace.

Savage Bushido has three arcane traditions. They share the basic SWADE framework — Arcane Backgrounds, Power Points, arcane skill rolls — but they differ in source, cost, recovery, and the social position they give their practitioners. A Hi-do Shugenja and a Buddhist Gakusho are not the same kind of person doing the same kind of thing with different labels. They are different kinds of practitioners with different relationships to different aspects of spiritual reality.

## The Three Traditions

### Shugendo — The Way of Mountain Asceticism

Shugendo is the elemental tradition — the channeling of raw natural forces through training, asceticism, and the techniques developed by the legendary En-no-Gyoja. Where Buddhism and Shinto work through relationship (with the Buddhas, with the Kami), Shugendo works through mastery. The Shugenja commands the elements.

**Arcane Skill:** Shugendo (Smarts)
**Recovery:** Standard SWADE — 1 PP per hour of rest; full recovery after 8 hours of sleep.
**Spiritual Cost:** None. Shugendo does not accumulate Pollution or Kegare.
**Social Position:** Marginal. Yamabushi (mountain practitioners) are respected for their power and feared for their strangeness. Their −2 On modifier reflects a life lived outside normal society.

*Protection* cast by a Buddhist Gakusho glows golden; the same power from a Shinto Gakusho shimmers with heat above summer stones. A Shugenja's *protection* crackles with elemental force appropriate to their School — fire-shield for Hi-do, water-skin for Mizu-do, stone-hide for Tsuchi-do.

**Overextension:** A Shugenja who attempts a power beyond their current PP may overextend. Make a Spirit roll (TN 4) after declaring the attempt:
- *With success:* The power works; the Shugenja takes 1 Fatigue.
- *With failure:* The power fails; the Shugenja takes 1 Fatigue.
- *With a critical failure:* The power fails, the Shugenja takes 2 Fatigue, and is Shaken for one hour (the backlash is significant).

### Buddhist Miracles — The Compassionate Power

Buddhist miracles flow from the practitioner's relationship with the Buddhas and Bodhisattvas — the enlightened beings who have chosen to remain accessible to those in suffering. The power is not the Gakusho's own; it is granted through faith, devotion, and the cultivation of compassion. A Buddhist Gakusho who acts contrary to Buddhist ethics finds their access to miracles compromised.

**Arcane Skill:** Butsudo (Spirit)
**Recovery:** Full PP recovery through sunrise devotion — a 30-minute practice of sutra recitation and meditation performed at dawn. Without this morning practice, PP recover at standard rate (1 per hour).
**Spiritual Cost:** Pollution (see below).
**Social Position:** High. Buddhist clergy carry significant social weight. The +2 On modifier reflects genuine respect for the tradition.

A Buddhist Gakusho who finds ways to resolve conflicts without killing — subdual combat, *sanctuary*, negotiation, escape — accumulates almost no Pollution. Their *barrier* is a wall of sacred flame. Their *bolt* is the lightning of enlightenment striking confusion.

**Overextension:** As Shugendo, plus 2 Pollution on a success, 1 Pollution on a failure, and maximum Pollution on a critical failure.

Oni are specifically resistant to Buddhist miracles that rely on the Buddha's compassion — *sanctuary*, *relief*, *healing* — as these do not touch creatures from Yomi. *Exorcism*, *smite* (holy trapping), and direct attack powers affect them normally.

### Shinto Miracles — The Kami's Favor

Shinto miracles arise from relationship with the Kami — the spirits of the natural world. The Shinto Gakusho does not command the Kami; they petition them, maintain the relationships through ritual purity and ceremony, and receive power as an expression of the Kami's favor. A Shinto Gakusho who accumulates Kegare finds the Kami less willing to respond.

**Arcane Skill:** Shinten (Spirit)
**Recovery:** Full PP recovery at a shrine or sacred site. Away from such sites, PP recover at standard rate. A Shinto Gakusho who is far from any shrine for an extended period is spiritually isolated in a meaningful way.
**Spiritual Cost:** Kegare (see below).
**Social Position:** High. Shrine priests carry authority similar to Buddhist clergy. +2 On modifier.

*Exorcism* affects all supernatural beings with a spirit presence, regardless of physical form. *Detect/conceal arcana* reveals supernatural beings, including those in spirit form, to the caster.

**Overextension:** As Shugendo, plus 2 Kegare on a success, 1 Kegare on a failure, and maximum Kegare on a critical failure.

## Pollution — Buddhist Spiritual Impurity

Pollution (kegare in the Buddhist context, though the term "Pollution" distinguishes it from the Shinto system) is the spiritual impurity that accumulates when a Buddhist Gakusho or Sohei comes into contact with things that cloud the Buddhist mind — death, violence, moral transgression, and spiritual corruption.

Pollution is not sin. It is not a moral judgment. A Buddhist Gakusho who kills in defense of the innocent still accumulates Pollution — the act itself leaves a residue, regardless of the justification. Clearing that residue requires ritual, not confession.

### Pollution Accumulation

| Source | Pollution Gained |
|---|---|
| Killing any living creature | +1 |
| Direct contact with a human corpse | +1 |
| Breaking a Buddhist precept | +1 |
| Missing sunrise devotion | +1 |
| Extended proximity to Oni or Yurei | +1 per hour |
| Overextension (see above) | +1 to +max |
| Casting certain darker powers | +1 to +2 (GM discretion) |

### Pollution Effects

| Pollution Level | Effect |
|---|---|
| 0 | No penalty |
| 1–2 | −1 to Butsudo rolls |
| 3–4 | −2 to Butsudo rolls. Sacred Texts blocked. |
| 5+ | −4 to Butsudo rolls. PP recovery halved. Visibly diminished — NPCs notice. |

### Clearing Pollution

- **Temple Ceremony:** One full day at a Buddhist temple, 1–5 Ryo depending on severity. Clears all Pollution.
- **Goma Ritual (Sacred Text):** The purification fire ritual. Takes three full rounds of concentration. Clears 1 Pollution per casting. Requires no temple — can be performed anywhere.
- **Sustained Purity:** A Gakusho who avoids all Pollution sources and performs sunrise devotion daily for seven days reduces Pollution by 1 per day.

## Kegare — Shinto Ritual Impurity

Kegare is the Shinto concept of ritual impurity — the disruption of the clean relationship between humanity and the Kami that occurs through contact with death, blood, illness, and transgression. Where Buddhist Pollution is about what one does, Kegare is also about what one is near. A Shinto Gakusho who attends a battlefield may accumulate Kegare simply by being present, without taking any action.

### Kegare Accumulation

| Source | Kegare Gained |
|---|---|
| Contact with a human or animal corpse | +1 |
| Witnessing or participating in violent death | +1 |
| Entering a space where someone has died recently | +1 |
| Contact with blood (own or others') | +1 |
| Serious breach of Shinto taboo | +1 to +2 |
| Extended proximity to Oni or Yurei | +1 per hour |
| Overextension | +1 to +max |

### Kegare Effects

| Kegare Level | Effect |
|---|---|
| 0 | No penalty |
| 1–2 | −1 to Shinten rolls |
| 3–4 | −2 to Shinten rolls. Sacred Texts blocked. Notice rolls to sense Kami become unreliable. |
| 5+ | −4 to Shinten rolls. PP recovery halved. The Kami withdraw — the Gakusho cannot sense spiritual presences at all. |

### Clearing Kegare

- **Misogi (Running Water Purification):** Stand in running water for 30 minutes while reciting purification prayers. Clears 1 Kegare. Can be performed anywhere with a river or stream.
- **Shrine Ceremony:** One full day at a Shinto shrine, 1–5 Ryo. Clears all Kegare.
- **Harae (Great Purification):** A formal ceremony performed by a Shinto Gakusho of Group Rank 3+. Clears all Kegare for the caster and up to five others. Takes one hour.

## Sacred Texts

Sacred Texts are specific religious documents — sutras, norito, ritual scripts — that a Gakusho may recite to activate a specific power at no PP cost. The power activates at success level only (no raise effect). Sacred Text activation requires one full uninterrupted round of recitation. No other actions may be taken.

**Once per scene per text.** A text cannot be recited twice in the same scene.

**Blocked at Pollution/Kegare 3+.** A spiritually impure Gakusho cannot access their Sacred Texts — the impurity interrupts the connection.

**Witnesses:** The recitation is audible and unmistakably religious. Anyone in earshot knows a religious ceremony is being performed.

### Buddhist Sacred Texts

| Sacred Text | Power Activated | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Heart Sutra | *protection* (self) | The most commonly known sutra |
| Healing Sutra | *healing* (touch) | One target within reach |
| Goma Ritual | *exorcism* | Three rounds of fire ceremony |
| Amida Sutra | *relief* | Removes Fatigue and minor ailments |
| Kannon Sutra | *sanctuary* | Three-round recitation |
| Fudo Mantra | *smite* (weapon, holy) | Weapon blazes with sacred fire |
| Lotus Sutra | *boost trait* (Spirit, self) | Thirty-minute recitation minimum |
| Prajna Sutra | *arcane protection* | One-hour preparation required |

### Shinto Sacred Texts (Norito)

| Sacred Text | Power Activated | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Oharae no Kotoba | *dispel* | The Great Purification norito |
| Healing Norito | *healing* (touch) | Shrine-specific, varies by Kami |
| Exorcism Norito | *exorcism* | +2 to Shinten roll for this activation |
| Ward Norito | *protection* (boundary) | Protects a space rather than a person |

## Power Recovery by Tradition

| Tradition | Standard Recovery | Enhanced Recovery | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shugendo | 1 PP/hour | Full on 8 hours sleep | No ritual required |
| Buddhist | 1 PP/hour | Full on sunrise devotion (30 min) | Devotion must be uninterrupted |
| Shinto | 1 PP/hour | Full at shrine or sacred site | Must be at the location |

**Sacred sites for Shinto recovery:** A recognized shrine, a sacred grove, a mountain with an associated Kami, a river source, or any location that the GM determines has significant spiritual presence. A Shinto Gakusho can identify potential sacred sites with a Notice roll (TN 4) in wilderness settings.

**Disrupted recovery:** Any interruption of sunrise devotion (for Buddhist Gakusho) prevents the enhanced recovery. An attack, an urgent summons, even a loud argument nearby may require a Spirit roll (TN 4) to maintain concentration.

## The Spirit World in Nippon

The spirit world is not a separate plane that characters travel to. It is the same world, seen differently. A location that appears as a forest in the mundane world appears as the domain of its forest Kami in the spirit-sight view. The spirit world interpenetrates the physical world at every point.

### Types of Spiritual Beings

**Kami (Minor and Major):** The spirits of natural features, ancestors, and abstract forces. Minor Kami are the spirits of specific trees, streams, and household features. Major Kami are the great deities of the Shinto pantheon — Amaterasu, Susanoo, Inari. Major Kami do not appear directly; they work through omens, through their minor servants, and through the prayers of their priests.

**Yurei (Ghosts):** The spirits of the dead who remain in the mortal world because of unresolved attachment — rage, grief, love, unfulfilled duty. Yurei are tied to specific locations or people. They cannot permanently be destroyed by combat alone — they must be laid to rest through addressing whatever holds them.

**Oni:** Demons from Yomi (the underworld). Unlike Yurei, Oni choose to be in the mortal world. They spread suffering, disease, and disaster. They are actively hostile and can be defeated — but their appearance signals that something has allowed them to cross over, and that source must be found.

**Kitsune, Tengu, Tanuki:** Supernatural beings that occupy the space between Kami and human — powerful, with their own agendas, neither straightforwardly good nor evil. Full descriptions in Appendix D.

### Interacting with the Spirit World

**Sensing spiritual presences:** Shinto Gakusho with Kami Sense detect supernatural presences automatically within range. Other characters may attempt Notice rolls (TN 6) to feel that something is spiritually significant about a location.

**Communicating with Kami:** The *speak with Kami* power enables direct communication. Without it, communication is indirect — through omens, through unusual events, through the behavior of animals associated with the Kami.

**Exorcism and the unquiet dead:** *Exorcism* works but should be a last resort — it is a violation of the Kami, not a resolution of the problem. A Yurei driven away by *exorcism* returns in 24 hours unless its underlying attachment is addressed. A Kami forcibly exorcised from its home does not forget.

**Pollution, Kegare, and the spirit world:** Spiritual impurity makes the spirit world harder to navigate. A Gakusho at Pollution/Kegare 4+ finds that their perception of spiritual presences becomes unreliable — they may fail to sense a Yurei or mistake an Oni for a lesser spirit. At 5+, the spiritual senses shut down entirely.

## The Onmyoji — Court Magicians

The Onmyoji are a separate tradition not available as a standard Profession but accessible through the Onmyoji Initiate Edge (Chapter Five). They practice Onmyodo — the Way of Yin and Yang — the court art of divination, geomancy, and astral timing that governs the calendars and ceremonies of the Imperial court.

Onmyoji are not priests. They do not serve the Kami or the Buddhas. They work with the abstract cosmic forces of Yin, Yang, and the Five Elements as a philosophical and divinatory system. Their powers cluster around perception, revelation, and temporal/spatial analysis rather than healing or combat.

**Arcane Skill:** Divination (Smarts)
**Starting powers:** *detect/conceal arcana*, *locate*, *farsight*
**Social position:** High — Onmyoji serve the court and are associated with Imperial authority.

The Onmyoji Initiate Edge is restricted to Kuge-born characters or those sponsored by an established Onmyoji. It represents entry into a specific institutional tradition, not merely the development of divination skills.

## Kamimusha — Warrior Priests of the Kami

Kamimusha are an organizational rank within the Shinto Shrine organization, not a Profession. A Gakusho (Shinto) who has achieved sufficient rank within a major shrine and taken on its warrior-protection role becomes a Kamimusha — a distinction recognized across Nippon.

Kamimusha are distinguished from Sohei in that they serve specific local Kami rather than the Buddhist institution. Their fighting style is typically more weapon-focused (naginata and yumi are traditional) and less focused on the Buddhist miracle powers that Sohei carry.

See Appendix A for the Shinto Shrine organization rank table and the conditions for becoming Kamimusha.

## Five Schools — Elemental Power Reference

The five Shugendo Schools and their elemental associations are described fully in Appendix C. The following table provides a quick reference for the character creation and play context.

| School | Element | Governing Concept | Natural Terrain |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hi-do | Fire | Transformation, purification, destruction | Volcanic mountains, forge-cities |
| Mizu-do | Water | Deception, dissolution, flow | Rivers, ocean coasts, rain country |
| Hayashi-do | Wood | Growth, truth, life | Deep forest, agricultural valleys |
| Kane-do | Metal | Control, precision, hardness | Mine country, castle cities |
| Tsuchi-do | Soil | Endurance, foundation, patience | Farmland, low mountains, river plains |

Each School has its own power list. A Shugenja may only access powers from their chosen School. The Yoga Scholar Edge (Chapter Five) allows Gakusho to access one School's Novice and Seasoned powers through religious practice.

## Playing an Arcane Character

A few practical notes for players and GMs running arcane practitioners in Savage Bushido.

**Track impurity separately.** Pollution and Kegare are separate trackers on the character sheet, distinct from Wounds and Fatigue. A Gakusho at Pollution 3 is not Wounded — they are spiritually compromised. The effects are real but different in character.

**The morning devotion matters.** A Buddhist Gakusho who consistently skips sunrise devotion will find their PP pool perpetually depleted. This is a feature, not a bug — the discipline of the tradition is mechanically enforced.

**Teachers change the game.** A Shugenja who finds a master of their School willing to teach Veteran-level powers gains access to a qualitatively different level of capability. The search for such teachers, and the service required to earn their instruction, is one of the driving adventure structures for arcane characters.

**The spirit world is not separate.** There is no "going to the spirit world" in the standard sense. The Kami, Yurei, and Oni that arcane characters deal with are present in the same physical locations as the mundane world. The difference is perception and access — a Shinto Gakusho at a shrine is at the center of a rich spiritual environment; a Shugenja deep in the mountains is similarly embedded in their elemental tradition. This embeddedness is what makes their powers work.
