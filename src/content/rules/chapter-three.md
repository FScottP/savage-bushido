Skills represent what your character has learned to do. Every skill is linked to an Attribute — when you make a skill roll, you roll that skill's die and your Wild Die (d6 for Wild Cards), taking the higher result.

Skills start at d4 and advance through d6, d8, d10, and d12. Raising a skill to its linked Attribute's die type or below costs 1 Advance point. Raising it above the linked Attribute costs 2 Advance points.

Savage Bushido uses 27 skills. Several differ from standard SWADE — Warfare replaces Knowledge (Battle), Socialize handles most formal social interaction, and the three arcane skills (Butsudo, Shugendo, Shinten) are unique to this setting. The three script skills (Katakana, Hiragana, Kanji) are also setting-specific.

---

## Athletics
**Linked Attribute:** Agility

Running, jumping, climbing, swimming, and all physical feats that require coordination and exertion. Also covers throwing weapons and catching objects.

In Nippon, Athletics is tested when crossing difficult terrain, scaling castle walls, leaping between rooftops, or swimming armored across a river. A warrior who neglects Athletics finds that the battlefield's geography matters as much as their sword arm.

---

## Boating
**Linked Attribute:** Agility

Operating watercraft — from fishing boats and river ferries to coastal trading vessels. Nippon is an island nation; its rivers, bays, and coastal waters are highways as much as the roads.

Characters without Boating may attempt basic boat handling at a penalty, but ocean crossings and combat aboard moving vessels require actual skill.

---

## Butsudo
**Linked Attribute:** Spirit

The arcane skill for Arcane Background (Miracles: Buddhist). Represents the ability to commune with the Buddhas and Bodhisattvas such that divine power is granted. Available only to Gakusho (Buddhist) and Sohei characters with the appropriate Arcane Background. Full rules in Chapter Eight: The Arcane.

Gakusho (Buddhist) who take the Gakusho Ki Profession Edge gain a dedicated 5-point Ki pool used for Devotion and Lore Master abilities. Gakusho who take the Yoga Scholar Edge may also cast Shugendo powers using Butsudo — see Chapter Four for the Gakusho Ki and Yoga Scholar Edges.

---

## Common Knowledge
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

General awareness of the world — history, geography, customs, current events, and the kind of information any reasonably attentive person picks up over a lifetime. In Nippon, Common Knowledge covers awareness of the major clans, the Shogunate's structure, religious customs, seasonal festivals, and basic social protocols.

Common Knowledge rolls are made when a character might plausibly know something without specialized training. For deeper expertise, use the appropriate Knowledge specialty instead.

---

## Craft
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

Making and repairing tangible objects. Skilled craftsmanship is as much knowledge as muscle — understanding materials, techniques, and the standards of quality that separate adequate work from excellent work.

Craft is a single skill that covers your specific craft specialty. A character chooses their specialty at character creation: swordsmith, armorer, potter, calligrapher, carpenter, weaver, etc. A swordsmith can attempt to repair a suit of armor but does so at a penalty.

**Material Quality** affects what can be created. Superior materials allow Superior results; inferior materials cap the quality achievable regardless of skill. The GM sets the available materials; the Craft roll determines how well the character uses what they have.

**Three-Layer Art System:** When Craft is used to create an artistic object — a poem, a calligraphic scroll, a lacquered sword handle — it interacts with the Arts of Nippon system described at the end of this chapter.

---

## Driving
**Linked Attribute:** Agility

Operating land vehicles — ox carts, palanquins, and similar conveyances. Rarely called on in standard adventuring but relevant for caravan escorts, noble travel, and some urban scenarios.

---

## Fighting
**Linked Attribute:** Agility

All melee combat — swords, spears, unarmed strikes, improvised weapons. Fighting is the single skill covering all close-quarters attacks regardless of weapon type.

What makes a warrior distinctive in Savage Bushido is not a separate sword skill — it is their Bugei Edges. Fighting determines whether they hit. Bugei Edges determine what happens when they do.

---

## Gambling
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

Games of chance and skill — dice (bakuchi), cards, and various traditional Nipponese gambling games. The Yakuza Profession begins with Gambling at d6 as a professional requirement.

Gambling can be used to generate income during downtime, to read opponents (as a social action in some contexts), or to create situations. A Game Master may use opposed Gambling rolls to determine the outcome of a high-stakes game that matters to the plot.

---

## Healing
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

Medical treatment — treating Wounds, illness, and poison. A character with Healing can attempt to stabilize an Incapacitated ally, treat injuries after combat, and identify the nature of diseases and toxins.

In Nippon, Healing encompasses both practical medicine (herbs, bandaging, setting bones) and the diagnostic traditions of Chinese medicine (pulse reading, elemental imbalance theory). Buddhist Gakusho also study Healing as a religious obligation, which creates occasional overlap with their Butsudo powers — the skill represents mundane medical knowledge; the power represents divine intervention.

With a success, a Healing roll removes one Wound from a patient who has rested at least one hour since being wounded. A raise removes two Wounds.

---

## Intimidation
**Linked Attribute:** Spirit

Forcing compliance through fear, implied threat, or the projection of overwhelming confidence. In Nippon, Intimidation is a blunt instrument — effective but socially costly when used openly. A Samurai who intimidates subordinates is exercising legitimate authority; a Samurai who intimidates equals or superiors risks serious On consequences.

Intimidation can be used as a social attack in the Tier 3 Social Conflict system (see Chapter Seven). With a success, the target is Distracted. With a raise, they are Distracted and Vulnerable until the end of their next turn.

---

## Investigation
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

Active research — searching locations, examining evidence, consulting records, questioning witnesses. Where Notice is passive awareness, Investigation is deliberate inquiry.

Investigation rolls are made to find hidden compartments, read the scene of a crime, trace the origin of a piece of armor, or determine who had access to a private document. The GM sets the Target Number based on how well-concealed the information is and how much time the character spends looking.

---

## Knowledge [Subject]
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

Specialized expertise in a particular field. Die level indicates depth: d4 (beginner) through d12 (master). The following subjects are available:

- **Knowledge (Buddhism):** Buddhist doctrine, temple hierarchy, ritual requirements, sacred texts, the history of Buddhism in Nippon
- **Knowledge (Shinto):** Shinto cosmology, Kami lore, shrine protocols, purification rituals, festival calendars
- **Knowledge (Chinese Classics):** Confucian and Taoist philosophy, Chinese history, classical poetry — the mark of a cultured Buke
- **Knowledge (Japanese Classics):** Nipponese poetry (waka), prose literature, court culture, the great sagas
- **Knowledge (Court Etiquette):** Formal protocol, precedence, gift-giving customs, modes of address — essential for anyone navigating the higher levels of Nipponese society
- **Knowledge (Clan Lore/Heraldry):** The genealogies, mon (crests), and histories of the great clans — who is related to whom, who owes what to whom
- **Knowledge (Medicine):** Classical Chinese medicine, herbal knowledge, and surgical technique beyond the basic Healing skill
- **Knowledge (Law):** The legal codes of the Shogunate, provincial customs, the rights and obligations of each social class
- **Knowledge (Navigation):** Coastal and ocean navigation, reading weather and stars, safe harbor knowledge
- **Knowledge (Agriculture):** Farming techniques, soil quality, water management, crop cycles — relevant for landed characters managing estates
- **Knowledge (Commerce):** Trade routes, commodity prices, merchant customs, negotiation practices
- **Knowledge (Supernatural Lore):** The nature and behavior of Kami, Oni, Yurei, and supernatural creatures — knowledge that Shugenja and Gakusho accumulate formally, and that rural people accumulate from experience
- **Knowledge (Poison):** The preparation, identification, and treatment of poisons. This knowledge is not illegal — but it attracts attention
- **Knowledge (Architecture/Fortification):** Castle design, defensive works, siege preparation — the military engineer's specialty
- **Knowledge (Divination):** Onmyodo — the court art of reading omens, auspicious timing, and geomantic analysis

A character may take Knowledge in any subject that makes sense for their background. These are the most common in Savage Bushido; the GM may approve others.

## Notice
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

Passive awareness — seeing, hearing, and sensing things without actively searching. Notice is rolled when the GM determines that a character might detect something: an ambush, a hidden weapon, a subtle emotional reaction, a fire burning somewhere nearby.

Notice is the skill most frequently rolled by the GM on behalf of characters (without announcing the roll). A failure doesn't mean the character actively failed to look — it means they simply didn't notice.

---

## Perform
**Linked Attribute:** Spirit

Artistic performance before an audience — music (biwa, shakuhachi, taiko), dance, poetry recitation, storytelling, and formal ceremony conducted as performance. Perform is the presentation layer of the Arts of Nippon system.

Perform rolls are made when a character performs for an audience whose reaction matters — entertaining a lord, performing at a ceremony, reciting a poem in a social conflict. A raise on a Perform roll creates a moment of genuine beauty or power that may shift the audience's Disposition in social interactions.

**Tea ceremony as performance:** A formal tea ceremony conducted with Perform skill rather than merely Socialize transforms the interaction from a social exchange into an artistic event. This changes the social conflict mechanics — see Chapter Seven.

---

## Riding
**Linked Attribute:** Agility

Controlling a horse (or other mount) in ordinary circumstances and under stress. Mounted combat, riding across difficult terrain, and keeping a warhorse steady under fire all require Riding rolls.

Buke characters are expected to ride. A Samurai who cannot ride competently loses social standing in military contexts. Heimin characters rarely ride unless their work requires it — merchants with pack horses may have Riding at d4 without social comment; farmers essentially never ride.

---

## Shinten
**Linked Attribute:** Spirit

The arcane skill for Arcane Background (Miracles: Shinto). Channeling divine power through relationship with the Kami and adherence to ritual purity. Available to Gakusho (Shinto) characters with the appropriate Arcane Background. Full rules in Chapter Eight: The Arcane.

Gakusho (Shinto) who take the Gakusho Ki Profession Edge gain a dedicated 5-point Ki pool for Devotion and Lore Master. Gakusho who take the Yoga Scholar Edge may cast Shugendo powers using Shinten — see Chapter Four.

---

## Shooting
**Linked Attribute:** Agility

All ranged attacks — bows (hankyu, daikyu, yumi), thrown weapons, and firearms (tanegashima arquebus). Like Fighting, the weapon type doesn't change the skill; the Bugei Edges (Kyujutsu, Teppojutsu) provide the specialized benefits.

---

## Shugendo
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

Channeling elemental forces through the teachings of En-no-Gyoja and the Five Schools. Available only to Shugenja characters. Full rules in Chapter Eight: The Arcane.

---

## Socialize
**Linked Attribute:** Spirit

Formal social interaction — the art of navigating Nipponese social protocol, reading interpersonal dynamics, making and receiving gifts appropriately, and building or maintaining relationships within the social hierarchy. Socialize is the primary skill for most social interactions in Nippon.

Socialize differs from standard SWADE Persuasion in important ways. It is not about emotional manipulation or raw charm — it is about demonstrating that you understand and respect the social forms. A Bushi who knows exactly how to address a Daimyo's household head, when to speak and when to remain silent, and how to make a gift that acknowledges obligation without creating awkwardness — that is Socialize.

**On modifier applies:** When a character makes a Socialize roll, their On modifier applies if the target knows their social standing. A character with On 15 (modifier +1) socializing with a lord who knows their reputation adds +1 to the roll.

**Group Rank modifier applies:** In interactions within or between organizations, Group Rank differences add or subtract from Socialize rolls. See Chapter Seven.

---

## Stealth
**Linked Attribute:** Agility

Moving without being detected — silently, in shadow, or by blending into a crowd. Stealth is also used to conceal items on your person and to tail someone without being noticed.

**Armor and Stealth:** Heavy armor imposes −2 to Stealth (Toughness bonus of +4 or more). Light armor imposes no penalty.

---

## Streetwise
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

Knowledge of the criminal underworld, black markets, and the informal information networks of urban life. Finding a fence, locating a safe house, identifying gang territory, and knowing who to bribe are all Streetwise actions.

In Nippon, Streetwise also covers the Yakuza information network — the shadow economy of favors, protection, and intelligence that the criminal organizations maintain alongside their legitimate fronts.

---

## Survival
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

Finding food, water, and shelter in the wilderness. Reading weather. Tracking people and animals. Knowing which plants are edible and which are poisonous. Navigating without roads or landmarks.

Survival is essential for Yamabushi (mountain sorcerers) and Shugenja who spend extended time in the wilderness. It is also relevant for any character who might be separated from civilization — a not-uncommon situation in a land of active military conflict.

---

## Taunt
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

Provoking, mocking, and verbally destabilizing opponents. In standard SWADE, a successful Taunt makes the target Distracted. In Savage Bushido, Taunt has an additional interaction with the On system.

**On Inversion:** When taunting someone of significantly higher On, the target gains +1 to resist per 3 points of On they are above you. When taunting someone of lower On, you gain the same bonus. A high-On character mocking a low-On character in public does so from a position of social authority — and that authority makes the taunt sting.

Taunt is also the skill used in the Challenge phase of the Formal Duel system. See Chapter Six.

---

## Warfare
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

Tactical and strategic military command. Warfare covers battlefield decision-making, reading an opponent's formation, positioning forces, siege planning, and the conduct of Mass Battle. It replaces Knowledge (Battle) and Knowledge (Strategy/Siege) from standard SWADE.

In the Mass Battle system (Chapter Six), Warfare rolls determine the outcome of large-scale engagements. A skilled Warfare character can shift the odds of a battle involving hundreds of soldiers through tactical insight alone.

## Script Skills

Literacy in Nippon is neither universal nor binary. The written language uses three distinct systems, each requiring separate training. These are treated as skills rather than simple yes/no knowledge, reflecting the depth of mastery required.

### Katakana
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

The basic syllabic script — 46 characters representing sounds. Used for foreign words, telegraphic notes, and basic signage. The minimum literacy standard for anyone who can read at all.

### Hiragana
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

The flowing syllabic script — also 46 characters, but more cursive in form. Used for poetry, literature, personal correspondence, and the informal written language of daily life. Characters with Hiragana can read most documents that are not official or scholarly in nature.

### Kanji
**Linked Attribute:** Smarts

The classical Chinese characters — thousands of them, though practical literacy requires mastery of several hundred. Used for official documents, religious texts, scholarly works, legal codes, and formal correspondence. A character without Kanji cannot read a Shogunate proclamation, a temple sutra, or a deed of land ownership. Kanji is the script of power.

**Starting scripts by Birth:** See Step Zero in Chapter Two. High Buke begin with all three scripts at d4. Middle and Low Buke begin with Katakana and Hiragana at d4. Merchants and Artisans begin with Katakana at d4. Farmers begin with no scripts.

## The Arts of Nippon — Three-Layer System

Art in Nippon is not decoration. A poem delivered at the right moment can shift a political negotiation. A sword handle whose lacquer work reflects the recipient's clan colors is a social statement as much as a weapon. Calligraphy of exceptional quality carries the character of its author into every room it enters.

The Arts of Nippon system uses three layers, each with its own mechanic:

### Layer One: Creation (Craft or Knowledge)

The physical making of the art object, or the intellectual composition of a piece. This layer uses the appropriate Craft specialty (for physical objects) or Knowledge (Japanese Classics) or Knowledge (Chinese Classics) (for literary composition).

**Material Quality** applies here. A swordsmith working with inferior steel cannot produce a Superior sword regardless of their Craft roll.

| Craft Roll | Quality |
|---|---|
| Failure | Inferior — functional but flawed |
| Success | Average — competent, unremarkable |
| Raise | Good — clearly skilled work |
| Two Raises | Superior — notable craft, worth preserving |
| Three Raises | Master — exceptional, will be remembered |

### Layer Two: Transcription (Craft or Kanji)

Copying, reproducing, or teaching a piece — taking Good or better work and making faithful reproductions, or creating instructional scrolls. Transcription requires at least a success on the appropriate Craft or Kanji roll. The quality of the transcription cannot exceed the quality of the original.

This layer matters most for scholars, temple copyists, and Gakusho who reproduce sacred texts.

### Layer Three: Performance (Perform)

Presenting the art to an audience in real time. A poem can be read silently (Creation only) or recited aloud (Performance). A sword can be displayed (Creation only) or drawn and presented in a formal ceremony (Performance). Performance is what transforms craft into social currency.

**Perform rolls in social contexts:** A successful Perform roll in a social interaction shifts the audience's Starting Disposition one step toward Friendly. A raise shifts it two steps. This applies once per scene — repeated performances of the same piece lose their impact.

**The tea ceremony** uses all three layers: the preparation of the tea space (Craft), the selection and arrangement of objects with appropriate meaning (Knowledge), and the conduct of the ceremony itself (Perform). A full tea ceremony that uses all three layers and achieves at least Good quality on each becomes a Tier 3 Social Conflict event. See Chapter Seven.
