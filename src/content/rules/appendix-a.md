Organizations in Nippon are not background flavor — they are mechanical resources. Your Group Rank within an organization determines what help you can call on, what authority you carry, and what obligations you owe. This appendix provides the full rank tables, title conventions, On requirements, and available resources for all eight major organization types.

Use these tables in conjunction with the Group Rank rules in Chapter Seven. The advancement mechanic is always the same — demonstrated deeds, On minimum, successful Tier 3 Social Conflict petition — but the specific titles, requirements, and resources differ by organization type.

---

## 1. The Samurai Clan (Buke no Ie)

The Samurai clan is the primary organizational structure of Buke life. Membership is usually by birth, though exceptional warriors may be adopted into a clan or taken on as retainers.

| Group Rank | Title | On Minimum | Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Ronin / Probationer | Any | None — on sufferance |
| 1 | Ashigaru / Gokenin (retainer) | On 4+ | Lodging, basic stipend (1 Ryo/month), clan introduction |
| 2 | Samurai (recognized retainer) | On 7+ | Stipend (2–3 Ryo/month), equipment loans, clan backing in disputes |
| 3 | Hatamoto (senior retainer) | On 10+ | Stipend (5+ Ryo/month), command of 5–20 soldiers, formal authority in clan territory |
| 4 | Gokenin Elder / Advisor | On 13+ | Significant stipend, command of 50–200 soldiers, policy influence, access to clan treasury |
| 5 | Daimyo / Clan Head | On 16+ | Full clan resources, command of entire military force, political authority over clan territory |

### Military Sub-Track

Within a clan, characters may hold military rank alongside their Group Rank. Military rank governs Mass Battle command authority:

| Military Rank | Command | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ashigaru Captain | 10–50 soldiers | Group Rank 2 minimum |
| Yoriki (lieutenant) | 50–200 soldiers | Group Rank 3 minimum |
| Bugyo (commissioner) | 200–1000 soldiers | Group Rank 4 minimum |
| General of the Army | Entire clan force | Group Rank 5 or direct appointment |

## 2. Yakuza Organization (Bakuto / Tekiya)

The Yakuza criminal organization has its own parallel hierarchy — as rigidly structured as any Samurai clan, with its own codes of loyalty, obligation, and advancement.

| Group Rank | Title | On Minimum | Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Unaffiliated (Ippan) | Any | None |
| 1 | Kobun (junior member) | On 3+ | Safe house access, basic criminal network |
| 2 | Wakashu (young adult member) | On 5+ | Criminal contacts, fencing network, protection in gang territory |
| 3 | Shatei-gashira (lieutenant) | On 7+ | Command of 5–15 Kobun, district resources, underworld authority |
| 4 | Wakagashira (underboss) | On 9+ | Command of entire operation segment, significant criminal resources, political connections |
| 5 | Oyabun (gang boss) | On 12+ | Complete organizational authority, extensive criminal network, political influence via corruption |

### The Face Edge and Criminal On

Within Yakuza social contexts, the Face Edge (Chapter Five) allows criminal On to function normally regardless of the social modifier penalty. A Yakuza Oyabun with On 12 operates at full On within the criminal world — their reputation is as real there as any Samurai's in court.

## 3. Buddhist Temple (Jiin)

Buddhist temple hierarchies vary by sect, but the following structure applies broadly across most major temple complexes in Nippon.

| Group Rank | Title | On Minimum | Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Lay Supporter (Danka) | Any | Access to temple ceremonies, basic hospitality |
| 1 | Novice Monk (Shami) | On 6+ | Temple lodging, instruction, access to library |
| 2 | Ordained Monk (Unsui) | On 9+ | Full temple resources, teaching authority over novices, access to sacred texts |
| 3 | Senior Monk / Instructor (Joza) | On 11+ | Authority over a sub-temple or teaching hall, access to restricted texts, formal scholarly recognition |
| 4 | Prior / Head Priest (Jushoku) | On 13+ | Authority over a temple complex, command of Sohei guard force, political voice |
| 5 | Abbot (Jushoku-cho / Kancho) | On 15+ | Complete authority over the temple and all subsidiary institutions, major political influence |

### Scholar and Sohei Parallel Tracks

Within the Buddhist temple, characters follow one of two tracks:

**Scholar Track:** Emphasizes Butsudo, Knowledge (Buddhism), and the sacred text system. Advancement gates on religious knowledge and spiritual purity.

**Sohei Track:** Emphasizes Fighting, military command, and the defense of the temple. Advancement gates on martial accomplishment and demonstrated ability to protect the institution.

Both tracks share the same Group Rank table but have different deed requirements for advancement.

## 4. Shinto Shrine (Jinja)

Shinto shrine hierarchies are more locally variable than Buddhist temple structures, but the following applies to major shrines.

| Group Rank | Title | On Minimum | Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Parishioner (Ujiko) | Any | Participation in shrine festivals, basic hospitality |
| 1 | Shrine Acolyte (Kannagi) | On 6+ | Shrine lodging, instruction in norito, Miko or Kannagi duties |
| 2 | Shrine Priest (Kannushi) | On 9+ | Full shrine resources, authority to perform ceremonies, access to sacred norito |
| 3 | Senior Priest (Guji) | On 11+ | Authority over shrine operations, access to restricted norito, formal recognition by regional shrines |
| 4 | Head Priest (So-Guji) | On 13+ | Authority over shrine and subsidiary institutions, Kamimusha command authority |
| 5 | Grand Priest / Imperial Shrine Rank | On 16+ | Political authority derived from shrine's national importance; Ise-level shrines answer only to the Emperor |

### Priest and Kamimusha Parallel Tracks

As the Buddhist temple has Scholar and Sohei tracks, the Shinto shrine has:

**Priest Track:** Emphasizes Shinten, ritual knowledge, and spiritual purity (Kegare management).

**Kamimusha Track:** The warrior-priest defenders of the shrine. Emphasizes Fighting, Naginatajutsu or Kyujutsu, and the protection of sacred spaces. Kamimusha are the Shinto equivalent of Sohei — distinguished by serving specific local Kami rather than Buddhist institutional authority.

## 5. Martial School — The Ryu

The Ryu is the martial school that teaches specific Bugei techniques. Unlike clan membership (which is usually by birth), Ryu membership is earned through demonstrated worthiness and formal acceptance by the school's master.

| Group Rank | Title | On Minimum | Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Prospect (Monjin) | Any | May observe open training; no access to school techniques |
| 1 | Student (Deshi) | On 5+ | Access to Category Omote training, school lodging during training periods |
| 2 | Practitioner (Monka) | On 7+ | Access to Specialist Edge training, introduction to other Ryu members nationally |
| 3 | Senior Practitioner (Jukuren-sha) | On 10+ | Access to Okuden training (teacher-gated individually), authority to teach Omote and Specialist levels |
| 4 | Instructor (Shihan) | On 12+ | Full access to all school techniques, authority to formally accept new students |
| 5 | Headmaster (Soke) | On 14+ | Custodian of the school's secret scrolls (makimono), final authority on what is taught and to whom |

### Finding and Joining a Ryu

A character who wishes to join a Ryu must first find it (see Chapter Nine, Finding Teachers), then petition for acceptance. The petition is a Tier 2 Social Conflict (SWADE Social Conflict rules) rather than Tier 3 — the Ryu headmaster is assessing competence and character, not engaging in political negotiation.

Acceptance as a Prospect (Group Rank 0) requires only demonstrating genuine interest and basic competence in the relevant Category Omote. Advancement from Prospect to Student (Group Rank 1) requires a demonstrated Specialist-level technique.

## 6. Elemental School / Mountain Retreat (Shugenja no Michi)

The elemental schools of Shugendo are less formally organized than temples or Ryu, but they have their own hierarchy of mastery.

| Group Rank | Title | On Minimum | Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Seeker (Gyoja) | Any | May train at mountain retreats; no formal affiliation |
| 1 | Initiate (Shugyo-sha) | On 4+ | Formal School affiliation, access to Novice powers, mountain retreat lodging |
| 2 | Practitioner (Shugenja) | On 6+ | Access to Seasoned powers, introduction to School network nationally |
| 3 | Master (Meijin) | On 9+ | Access to Veteran powers with appropriate teacher, authority to teach Novice and Seasoned levels |
| 4 | Grand Master (Dai-Meijin) | On 12+ | Access to Heroic powers, authority to teach all levels, significant spiritual reputation |
| 5 | School Patriarch (Soke) | On 15+ | Custodian of the School's secret techniques, final authority over the tradition |

### Cross-School Relations

The five elemental Schools are not formally allied but share a mutual respect based on the common foundation of Shugendo practice. A Shugenja of Group Rank 3+ in one School is treated as Group Rank 1 by other Schools for the purpose of hospitality and basic access — not for technique access, which remains School-specific.

## 7. Merchant House (Shōnin no Ie)

The great merchant houses of Nippon operate their own hierarchies, built on commercial success rather than martial or religious achievement.

| Group Rank | Title | On Minimum | Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Customer / Contractor | Any | Access to goods at standard prices |
| 1 | Apprentice (Detchi) | On 4+ | Trade training, house lodging, access to trade networks at preferred rates |
| 2 | Junior Clerk (Tedai) | On 5+ | Authority to conduct transactions on house behalf, access to house capital for business purposes |
| 3 | Senior Clerk / Manager (Banto) | On 7+ | Management authority over house operations in a region, significant commercial resources |
| 4 | Partner (Kogashira) | On 9+ | Profit-sharing, authority over major commercial decisions, political connections via commerce |
| 5 | House Master (Shonin-gashira) | On 11+ | Complete authority over the merchant house and all its operations |

### Commerce and On

Merchants officially have lower On than Buke, but commercial success creates its own form of influence. A Merchant House at Group Rank 4 or 5 can open doors that sword skills cannot — through loans, commercial relationships, and the fundamental reality that every army needs rice and every Daimyo needs funds.

## 8. City Police (Machi-bugyo-sho — The Magistrate's Office)

The city police organization combines judicial, investigative, and law enforcement functions under the Machi-bugyo (Town Magistrate).

| Group Rank | Title | On Minimum | Resources |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Civilian informant (Meakashi) | Any | No formal standing; paid per tip |
| 1 | Doshin (Police Officer) | On 6+ | Jitte (badge of authority), arrest rights over Heimin, district patrol assignment |
| 2 | Senior Doshin | On 8+ | Authority over junior Doshin, investigation authority, access to magistrate's records |
| 3 | Yoriki (Lieutenant) | On 10+ | Command of a district's Doshin force, formal judicial authority in minor cases |
| 4 | Deputy Magistrate (Fudai-bugyo) | On 12+ | Judicial authority in significant cases, command of the city's police force |
| 5 | Town Magistrate (Machi-bugyo) | On 14+ | Full judicial and police authority over the city, reports directly to the Shogunate |
