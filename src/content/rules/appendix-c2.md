Buddhist miracles flow from the practitioner's relationship with the Buddhas and Bodhisattvas. The power is not the Gakusho's own — it is granted through faith, devotion, and the cultivation of compassion. All Buddhist powers use the Butsudo skill (Spirit). Full PP recovery requires sunrise devotion (30 minutes of sutra recitation and meditation at dawn). Pollution accumulates through violence, death contact, and precept violation — see Chapter Eight for full rules.

## Core Principles of Buddhist Power

**Compassion as foundation:** Buddhist miracles work through compassion — the recognition that all beings suffer and that reducing suffering is the highest act. Powers that cause direct harm to sentient beings accumulate more Pollution than those that heal or protect. Oni and demons are not sentient in the Buddhist sense — direct powers against them accumulate no Pollution.

**Ritual context:** Many Buddhist powers have longer casting times when used in their full ritual form. The Sacred Texts system (Chapter Eight) provides abbreviated activation for specific powers at no PP cost — but only at success level.

**Pollution and access:** At Pollution 3+, Sacred Texts are blocked. At Pollution 5+, all Butsudo rolls suffer −4 and PP recovery is halved. Managing Pollution is as important as managing Power Points.

**Oni resistance:** Oni are specifically resistant to Buddhist miracles of compassion — *sanctuary*, *relief*, *healing* — as these do not touch creatures from Yomi. *Exorcism*, *smite* (holy trapping), Holy Aura, and Spear Against Spirits work normally against Oni.

## Buddhist Power Descriptions

### Sanctuary
*Novice, 2 PP, Self, 5 rounds*

New Power. The caster creates a zone of spiritual peace in a 3" radius centered on themselves. Within this zone:

- Hostile creatures must make a Spirit roll (TN 4) each round to take violent action against any target within the zone. Failure means they cannot attack that round.
- Oni and creatures of pure evil (GM's determination) must make a Spirit roll (TN 6) to enter the zone at all.
- All characters within the zone gain +2 to Spirit rolls to resist Fear.
- The caster may move while maintaining Sanctuary — the zone moves with them.

**Oni resistance:** Sanctuary does not function against Oni — their nature is antithetical to Buddhist compassion. The Oni rolls normally and ignores the zone. This is one of the few cases where the power explicitly fails against a target type.

**Maintaining:** Standard SWADE maintenance rules. The caster may not cast other powers while maintaining Sanctuary without incurring multi-action penalties.

---

### Pure Thought
*Seasoned, 3 PP, Smarts", Instant*

New Power. Targeted *dispel* specifically against mental and spiritual afflictions. Pure Thought removes:

- Active *confusion*, *puppet*, *slumber*, *fear* powers affecting the target
- Lower Trait effects targeting Spirit or Smarts
- Compulsions imposed by supernatural beings (Kitsune charm, Araburu-kami influence)
- The Distracted condition if caused by a supernatural source

Make an opposed Butsudo roll against the original casting roll of the affliction. With success, one affliction is removed. With a raise, all qualifying afflictions are removed simultaneously.

Pure Thought does not remove Wounds, Fatigue, Pollution, or mundane Hindrances. It removes only supernatural mental and spiritual interference.

---

### Exorcism
*Seasoned, 3 PP, Smarts", Special*

New Power. *Exorcism* drives supernatural beings from the mortal world — or, in the case of Yurei, severs their attachment to a specific location temporarily.

**Against Oni:** Opposed Butsudo vs. Spirit. With success, the Oni is pushed back 3" and cannot advance toward the caster for 3 rounds. With a raise, the Oni is Incapacitated (forced back to Yomi) for the remainder of the scene. Cannot permanently destroy an Oni — they may return in a future session.

**Against Yurei:** Opposed Butsudo vs. Spirit. With success, the Yurei is driven from the location for 24 hours. With a raise, for one week. The Yurei returns when the duration expires unless its Unfinished Business has been resolved. *Exorcism* alone cannot permanently lay a Yurei to rest — only addressing the underlying attachment can do that.

**Against Kitsune, Tengu, Tanuki:** Opposed Butsudo vs. Spirit. With success, the being is compelled to leave the immediate area (within 10"). With a raise, they may not return for the remainder of the scene.

**Against Araburu-kami:** +2 Spirit to resist (it belongs in the mortal world — it is not an intruder). Success drives it back 5" and suppresses its powers for 3 rounds. With a raise, suppresses for the scene. Does not resolve the underlying rage that created the Araburu-kami.

**Three-round ritual form (Sacred Text — Goma Ritual):** If used as a Sacred Text activation, the Exorcism takes three full rounds but gains +2 to the Butsudo roll.

---

### Holy Aura
*Seasoned, 4 PP, Self, 5 rounds*

New Power. The caster radiates a visible golden aura of spiritual protection.

- All supernatural evil beings (Oni, Araburu-kami, malevolent Yurei) must make a Spirit roll (TN 4) before they can attack the caster directly. Failure means they target someone else that round.
- The caster gains +2 Armor against attacks from supernatural evil sources.
- All allies within 3" gain +1 to Spirit rolls against Fear caused by supernatural beings.
- The aura is unmistakable — any supernatural being within Smarts" is immediately aware of the caster and their power level.

Holy Aura does not function against mundane human opponents — only against beings of supernatural evil origin.

---

### Spear Against Spirits
*Seasoned, 2 PP, Touch, 5 rounds*

New Power. Sanctifies one weapon so that it can affect ethereal beings — Yurei in manifested form, spirits, and other creatures that are normally immune to mundane weapons.

- The sanctified weapon deals full damage to ethereal beings.
- Against Oni and supernatural evil beings, the weapon deals +1d4 holy damage on a raise.
- The sanctification is visible as a faint golden glow along the blade or weapon head.
- One weapon only — the power must be recast for each weapon.
- If the weapon leaves the hands of the person it was cast on, the sanctification ends immediately.

This power is cast on a weapon, not a target. It uses standard *smite* targeting mechanics — touch range, the weapon must be held.

### Mass Sanctuary
*Veteran, 5 PP, Smarts", 5 rounds*

New Power. As Sanctuary but extended to cover up to six designated targets rather than just the caster. The zone does not move — it is centered on the casting location and remains there. All six targets receive the Sanctuary benefits while within the 3" radius. Targets who leave the area lose the benefit; targets who return regain it.

The caster may be one of the six designated targets. If the caster moves outside the zone, the zone remains — it does not follow them as standard Sanctuary does.

**Pollution note:** Mass Sanctuary accumulates Pollution at the standard rate — the scale does not increase the spiritual cost.

### Fetters of Truth
*Veteran, 4 PP, Smarts", Special*

New Power. Opposed Butsudo vs. Spirit. With success, the target is bound by spiritual obligation for one scene:

- They cannot make statements they know to be false. Attempted lies cause immediate Fatigue (1 level) and the lie fails — the target's mouth simply forms true words.
- They cannot initiate violence against the caster or any character the caster designates as protected.
- They may remain silent rather than answering a question — but they cannot deceive through action, gesture, or implication.

With a raise, the binding lasts until the next sunrise rather than one scene.

**Moral weight:** Using *divine mandate* to compel an action the relevant Kami would not sanction causes immediate Kegare +2. Using Fetters of Truth to extract information rather than prevent harm is a Minor Buddhist precept violation — +1 Pollution.

The bound target knows they are bound. They can feel the spiritual obligation. Resenting it while complying is entirely possible.

### Divine Mandate
*Veteran, 4 PP, Smarts", Special*

New Power. Compels a Kami, Yurei, or supernatural being of equal or lesser Spirit to perform one specific, finite service.

**Targeting:** Cannot target Oni (their nature resists Buddhist authority) or beings with Spirit higher than the caster's Butsudo die type.

**Mechanism:** Opposed Butsudo vs. Spirit. With success, the being is compelled to perform one clearly defined, finite service — deliver a message, cease haunting a location for one month, guide the party to a specific place. The service must be possible for the being to perform. The being cannot be commanded to act against its fundamental nature.

**Completion:** When the service is complete, the compulsion ends and the being is free. If the service is impossible (the caster asks the Yurei to leave a location it is bound to by Unfinished Business), the power fails even on a success — the binding cannot override the deeper spiritual reality.

**The Kami's response:** Using *divine mandate* to compel a Kami is spiritually violent — the Gakusho is overriding the Kami's will rather than working with it. The Kami performs the service but does not forget. Future interactions with that Kami will begin at Hostile Disposition. Repeated use of *divine mandate* against Kami will eventually result in the Kami seeking redress through other means.

The target does not remember being commanded. The compulsion works at a level below conscious memory.

### True Resurrection
*Heroic, 10 PP, Touch, Instant*

New Power. Returns a dead character to life. Requirements:

- The character must have died within a number of days equal to the caster's Spirit die type.
- The Buddhist funeral rites (Segaki ceremony) must not have been performed — once Segaki is complete, the spirit has moved on and cannot be recalled.
- The character must have died of causes other than extreme physical destruction (decapitation, complete incineration, etc. — GM's discretion on what constitutes unrestorable death).

**Mechanism:** Extended ritual taking one hour. The caster makes a Butsudo roll (TN 6). With success, the character returns to life at 0 Wounds (Incapacitated but alive). With a raise, they return with 1 Wound removed. With a failure, the ritual fails — the spirit cannot be reached or will not return. On a critical failure, the caster takes 3 Fatigue from the effort.

**Cost:** +3 Pollution immediately. This is the highest Pollution cost in the Buddhist tradition — the act of reversing death violates the natural order in a way that leaves a lasting spiritual mark.

**The returned character:** Returns with full memory intact. May have experienced something of the afterlife — the GM determines whether this is revealed and in what form. Characters returned by True Resurrection sometimes behave subtly differently, though this is a narrative choice rather than a mechanical one.

### Voice of the Buddha
*Heroic, 6 PP, Special, Special*

New Power. Once per session. The caster speaks a truth — a single statement about the nature of things, the consequences of actions, or the reality of a situation.

**Mechanism:** All characters within hearing (up to Smarts × 10 yards) who understand the language make a Spirit roll (TN 8). With failure, they cannot take hostile action against the caster or any character the caster designates as protected for the remainder of the scene. They are not Puppet-ed — they retain full agency for all other actions. They simply cannot bring themselves to act against the protected characters.

**The truth:** The statement must be genuinely true — the GM determines whether it qualifies. "You cannot defeat us" is not a truth. "You will carry the memory of this day for the rest of your life" is a truth. "This violence serves no one here" is a truth. The power does not function if the caster speaks something they know to be false or even partially misleading.

**Supernatural beings:** Oni are immune. Yurei make the Spirit roll at −2 (they are already caught between worlds — the voice resonates with something in them). Kitsune and Tengu make the roll normally.

**Pollution:** No Pollution accrues from this power — it is the purest expression of Buddhist truth-speaking.

## Sohei Power Access

Sohei have access to the Buddhist power list but begin with only 2 starting powers rather than 3. They may advance through the Buddhist power list normally as they gain rank. The following powers are particularly appropriate for Sohei given their warrior-monk role:

**Recommended Sohei starting powers:** *healing* (to sustain the temple community), *protection* (to survive the fight), *smite* via Compassionate Strike (to fight effectively).

**Sohei and Pollution:** Sohei accumulate Pollution at half rate when engaged in direct defense of the temple, its members, or innocent bystanders under direct threat. This reduced rate does not apply to offensive operations — a Sohei leading a raid on a rival temple accumulates Pollution at the normal rate.

**Sohei and Sanctuary:** Sohei may cast Sanctuary but the zone does not move with them — they are warriors and their movement is often more important than maintaining a stationary zone. A Sohei-cast Sanctuary remains centered on the casting location.
