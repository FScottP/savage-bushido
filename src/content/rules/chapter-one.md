The blade catches the afternoon light as it clears the scabbard. Across the garden, the Daimyo's tea cup is halfway to his lips. Neither man speaks. The poem composed this morning is still drying in the poet's quarters, waiting to be presented. The battle for the northern province has been fought; who won is still being negotiated. Somewhere in the hills, a Shugenja has been walking through fire for three days and counting. And in the city below, a Yakuza lieutenant is about to make the worst decision of a fairly poor life.

Welcome to Nippon. It is a world of extraordinary beauty and extraordinary violence, where the way you carry a sword and the way you pour tea are equally martial arts — one trained in the dojo, the other at court, but both capable of settling a life's worth of questions in a single moment. This is Savage Bushido.

## What Is Savage Bushido?

Savage Bushido is a complete tabletop roleplaying game setting built on the Savage Worlds Adventure Edition (SWADE) rules. It adapts the classic 1981 Bushido RPG by Bob Charrette and Paul Hume — one of the first RPGs to explore Japanese-inspired fantasy — into the modern Savage Worlds framework.

The original Bushido was remarkable for its time. It gave players a deeply considered vision of warring-states Japan: the strict social hierarchy, the layered martial arts system, the spiritual world that breathed behind every mountain and river, the weight of obligation and honor that shaped every human relationship. That vision is worth preserving and updating.

Savage Bushido preserves what made the original great — the On honor system, the Profession-based advancement, the multi-tier Bugei martial arts, the layered social conflict, the three arcane traditions — while using SWADE's fast, dramatic, and flexible mechanics to make it play more fluidly at the modern table.

### What Savage Bushido Is Not

Savage Bushido is not a history book. The world of Nippon described in these pages is a mythical Japan — inspired by medieval Japanese history and culture, but deliberately changed to create a fiction that belongs to the game rather than the historical record. The names of clans, Shoguns, and events have been altered. The current political conflict is inspired by the English Wars of the Roses as much as by Japanese history. The religions draw on Shinto and Buddhist traditions but simplify and adapt them for playability.

This approach serves two purposes. It allows players who know their Japanese history to engage with the setting on its own terms without being able to predict events from historical knowledge. And it gives Game Masters creative freedom to develop the world without being constrained by the historical record or worried about misrepresenting a living culture.

The goal is a world that feels authentically Japanese in texture and spirit, while being fully available to anyone who sits down to play — regardless of prior knowledge.

## For Players of the Original Bushido

If you played the original Bushido RPG — or if you have it sitting on your shelf and love what it was trying to do — this section is for you.

Savage Bushido is faithful to Bushido's vision. Every major concept from the original has been translated into SWADE terms:

- **On (Honor/Social Standing):** Preserved as a 1–20 scale that modifies social rolls and changes through play.
- **Professions:** All seven original Professions (Bushi, Budoka, Gakusho Buddhist, Gakusho Shinto, Sohei, Shugenja, Yakuza) are present with full advancement chains.
- **Bugei and Okuden:** The martial arts system is expanded into a clean three-tier structure. Category Omote Edges (broad families) → Specialist Edges (specific weapons) → Okuden (secret techniques requiring in-play teacher relationships).
- **MNA and Zanshin:** Translated into the Zanshin chain of Edges — a universal progression available to all Professions that reduces multi-action penalties from −2/−4 toward zero.
- **Ki Powers:** The Bushi's focused combat powers are preserved as a dedicated Ki Power Point pool activatable as free actions.
- **The Five Schools:** All five elemental schools (Hi-do, Mizu-do, Hayashi-do, Kane-do, Tsuchi-do) are preserved, with every Bushido spell mapped to a SWADE power or written as a new power in SWADE format.
- **Buddhist and Shinto Miracles:** Both religious traditions have distinct mechanics — Pollution vs. Kegare, different recovery methods, Sacred Texts — that feel like two different spiritual paths.
- **Social Conflict:** The detailed social interaction system from original Bushido has been expanded into a full three-tier social conflict system.

Some things are simplified by design. The original Bushido's granular derived statistics are replaced by SWADE's cleaner Attribute system. The complex level-by-level advancement tables are replaced by SWADE's Edge-based advancement. The intent is to capture the feel of the original while making the game faster to prepare, faster to play, and more accessible to players who haven't read a 200-page rules supplement.

Where the original Bushido was meticulous, Savage Bushido is dramatic. Both are legitimate approaches. We chose drama.

## For Players of Savage Worlds

If you're a Savage Worlds veteran coming to Savage Bushido from other SWADE settings, you'll find the core you know — Action Cards, Wild Cards, Bennies, the same dice mechanics you've internalized — plus a substantial set of setting rules that make Nippon feel genuinely different.

The most important things to know:

- **On is always in the room.** Your character's On score follows them everywhere. It affects social rolls, shapes how NPCs treat you, rises through honorable action and falls through disgrace, and interacts with the Group Rank system within your organization.
- **Bugei Edges are Edges, not skills.** Fighting covers all melee combat. What makes a Samurai different from a farmer with a sword is their Bugei Edges — the Tier 1 Category Omote, Tier 2 Specialist, and Tier 3 Okuden that unlock special maneuvers, passive bonuses, and ultimately secret techniques.
- **Okuden require teachers.** The most powerful martial techniques cannot simply be purchased as Advances. You must find a qualified teacher, earn their acceptance, and complete the study. Finding and satisfying teachers is part of the game, not a formality.
- **Social conflict is a system.** Chapter Seven contains a full Tier 3 social conflict system for high-stakes interactions — petitioning a Daimyo, a formal tea ceremony as political confrontation, a formal apology. These are structured encounters with momentum, disposition, and consequences.
- **The arcane traditions feel different.** A Shugenja with Hi-do (Fire School) and a Buddhist Gakusho are not just different arcane skill names — they have different recovery methods, different spiritual costs, different power lists, and different social positions.
- **Zanshin is for everyone.** The Zanshin multi-action penalty reduction chain is not restricted to fighters. A courtier who masters Socialize and Spirit can achieve Zanshin Kanzen just as validly as a swordsman.

## What You Need to Play

**Required:** Savage Worlds Adventure Edition (SWADE) Core Rules. Savage Bushido is a setting book — it assumes you have the core rules and builds on them.

**Helpful but not required:** The original 1981 Bushido RPG by Bob Charrette and Paul Hume. Players who have it will find many deliberate resonances in this book. Players who don't will not miss anything necessary.

**Standard SWADE supplies:** A standard deck of playing cards (both Jokers included) for Action Cards; a full set of polyhedral dice; tokens for Bennies; tokens for Wounds and Fatigue; something to track Power Points.

## Design Philosophy

Six principles guided every decision in building Savage Bushido.

### 1. SWADE Mechanics Wherever Possible

Every time a Bushido concept could be expressed through existing SWADE mechanics with good results, we used them. New mechanics are reserved for places where SWADE genuinely couldn't capture the original game's feel.

### 2. Trappings and Flavor First

A Hi-do bolt is mechanically a SWADE *bolt* power with a fire trapping. We added new mechanics only where trappings were insufficient — where the Bushido concept was genuinely mechanically distinct, not just visually different.

### 3. Consequences, Not Prohibitions

Nippon has a rigid social hierarchy, but Savage Bushido expresses it through consequences rather than hard locks. A Heimin can learn Kenjutsu — but it costs On. The rules don't prevent transgression; they make transgression meaningful.

### 4. Teacher-Gating Creates Story

Requiring in-play teachers for Okuden techniques is a deliberate design pillar. It creates relationships, adventures, and character arcs that would not otherwise exist. The search for a master, the service required to earn their trust, the journey to prove worthiness — these are the stories that Nippon is built for.

### 5. Social Consequences Are Real

On is not decorative. Group Rank is not background flavor. A character who loses On through dishonorable action is measurably less effective in social situations. The social systems are mechanical systems — which means social play matters as much as combat at the table.

### 6. The World Breathes

Nippon's political crisis exists independently of the player characters. The Shikken is tightening his grip. Yorike is maneuvering. The Ryojii-Wada war is escalating. Run Nippon as a living world, not a backdrop.

## Setting Rules Summary

The following setting rules modify standard SWADE. Players and GMs should review these before the first session.

| Setting Rule | What It Does |
|---|---|
| On (Honor Score) | Tracks social reputation on a 1–20 scale. Modifies social skill rolls. Changes through play. |
| Bugei Three-Tier System | Martial arts are Edges. Category Omote → Specialist → Okuden (teacher-gated). |
| Formal Duel | Three-phase structure: Challenge, Compact, Combat. Iai-jutsu acts before Action Cards in sheathed-start duels. |
| Subdual Damage | Declare before rolling. Melee only. Deals Fatigue rather than Wounds. |
| Pollution (Buddhist) / Kegare (Shinto) | Spiritual impurity penalizes arcane skill rolls. Cleared through religious ritual. |
| Sacred Texts | Activate specific powers at no PP cost. One full round. Once per scene per text. Blocked at Pollution/Kegare 3+. |
| Power Recovery by Tradition | Shugenja: standard SWADE. Buddhist Gakusho: full recovery on sunrise devotion. Shinto Gakusho: full recovery at shrine. |
| Armor Encumbrance | Each die type below Minimum Strength imposes −1 to Pace, Agility, and Agility-linked skills. |
| Group Rank (0–5) | Position within an organization. Determines resources, authority, and Social Status. Advances through petition and demonstrated deeds. |
| Zanshin Chain | Universal multi-action penalty reduction. Mushin (Novice) through Zanshin Kanzen (Legendary). Teachers required from Zanshin onward. |

## How to Use This Book

### For Players

Read Chapter Two in full before your first session — it is the backbone of everything. Read Chapter Three before finalizing your character to understand what skills are available. Read your chosen Profession's section in Chapter Four. Skim Chapter Five for the full Bugei and Okuden lists. Read Chapter Seven's section on On and the Gift Economy.

### For Game Masters

Read Chapter Nine first. Understanding the political landscape gives you the frame for every adventure you'll run. Read Chapter Seven carefully — the three-tier social system requires GM judgment. Appendix A is a working reference for when players join organizations. Appendix D provides baseline stat blocks.

## A Final Note

We hope Savage Bushido gives you a world worth caring about. A world where the poem you compose for a lord matters as much as how you handle a sword. Where the teacher who finally agrees to instruct you in Iai-jutsu becomes a character you remember. Where losing On because you made the right choice instead of the socially correct one creates a story you'll tell for years.

Now go find out what kind of Samurai you are.
