Creating a character in Savage Bushido follows nine steps. Work through them in order the first time. Experienced players will find steps they can handle simultaneously, but the sequence matters — choices made in Steps Zero and One shape everything that follows.

## Step Zero: Birth

Before you choose a Profession, you choose where your character comes from. Birth determines your starting social class, your initial On score, your script skills, and which broad category of Bugei your family trained you in from childhood.

Birth is not destiny. A Heimin can become a legendary warrior. A Buke can disgrace themselves into poverty. But Birth shapes your starting point — the assumptions the world makes about you before you've done anything to change them.

### Birth Table

Roll 2d6 or choose from the following:

| Roll | Birth | Starting On | Class | Scripts | Bugei Category |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2 | Ronin by Birth | 4 | Buke (marginal) | Katakana | Any one Omote |
| 3–4 | Low Buke | 6 | Buke | Katakana, Hiragana | Edged or Pole Weapons |
| 5–6 | Middle Buke | 8 | Buke | Katakana, Hiragana | Any one Omote |
| 7 | High Buke | 10 | Buke | All three scripts | Any two Omote |
| 8–9 | Merchant (Akindo) | 5 | Heimin | Katakana | Unarmed or Peasant |
| 10–11 | Artisan (Shokunin) | 5 | Heimin | Katakana | Unarmed or Peasant |
| 12 | Farmer (Hyakusho) | 4 | Heimin | None | Peasant Weapons |

**Buke** characters begin play with On equal to their Birth On. **Heimin** characters begin with On equal to their Birth On. All characters track On using the same 1–20 scale regardless of origin.

### What Birth Gives You

**On Score:** Your starting social reputation. This number will change constantly through play — rising through honorable action, falling through disgrace. Record it prominently on your character sheet.

**Social Class:** Buke are the warrior class. They may carry swords legally, are addressed with titles, and are subject to On loss for behavior unbecoming their station. Heimin are commoners. They have more freedom in some respects and less in others.

**Script Skills:** Literacy in Nippon is not universal. Katakana is the basic syllabic script used for simple reading and foreign words. Hiragana is the flowing script of poetry and literature. Kanji are the classical Chinese characters used for formal documents, religious texts, and scholarly work. Each script is a separate skill starting at d4.

**Bugei Category (Omote):** Every person in Nippon grows up learning the basics of some martial tradition, even if only in passing. Your Birth grants you one (or two, for High Buke) Category Omote Edge from the Bugei system — the broad foundation of your martial heritage. These are free; you do not spend Edge selections on Birth Bugei.

### Dual Heritage

With Game Master approval, a character may take a mixed Birth — the child of a Buke father and merchant mother, for example, or a Heimin who was adopted into a low Buke family. In these cases, take the lower On score and the scripts of the higher-status parent, and discuss with your GM what social complications this creates.

## Step One: Choose a Profession

Your Profession is your path — the martial, religious, criminal, or mystical tradition you have formally entered. It shapes your starting skills, your advancement options, and the organizations available to you.

There are seven Professions in Savage Bushido:

| Profession | Core Identity | Arcane? |
|---|---|---|
| Bushi | The warrior of the Buke class | Ki powers only |
| Budoka | The martial artist — unarmed and peasant weapons | Ki powers only |
| Gakusho (Buddhist) | The Buddhist monk or priest | Buddhist Miracles |
| Gakusho (Shinto) | The Shinto priest or shrine keeper | Shinto Miracles |
| Sohei | The warrior monk — combines Bushi and Gakusho | Buddhist Miracles |
| Shugenja | The elemental sorcerer of the mountain schools | Shugendo |
| Yakuza | The criminal — gambler, enforcer, gang member | None |

Each Profession has a full advancement chain described in Chapter Four. Your Profession determines which Edges are available to you as you advance, which organizations you can join, and how the world treats you socially.

### Yin and Yang — Dual Professions

A character may begin with two Professions if the combination makes narrative sense and the GM approves. This is called the Yin/Yang option. The character gains the starting benefits of both Professions but begins with fewer free skill points and must manage the obligations of both paths.

Common dual combinations: Bushi/Gakusho (the warrior-monk path, different from Sohei), Budoka/Yakuza (the criminal martial artist), Shugenja/Gakusho (the scholar-sorcerer). Discuss with your GM before choosing this option.

## Step Two: Attributes

You have five Attributes. Each starts at d4. You have five points to spend raising them. Each raise costs one point and moves the die one step up the chain: d4 → d6 → d8 → d10 → d12.

| Attribute | Covers |
|---|---|
| Agility | Speed, coordination, reflexes, acrobatics |
| Smarts | Intelligence, memory, perception, tactical thinking |
| Spirit | Willpower, faith, force of personality, Ki and arcane power |
| Strength | Raw physical power, melee damage |
| Vigor | Toughness, endurance, resistance to damage and fatigue |

No Attribute may be raised above d12 during character creation.

### Attributes and Your Profession

Different Professions emphasize different Attributes. Bushi and Budoka invest heavily in Agility and Strength. Shugenja need Smarts for Shugendo. Gakusho need Spirit for Butsudo and Shinten. Yakuza benefit from Smarts and Spirit equally. There are no wrong choices — a Spirit-focused Bushi who relies on Ki is as valid as an Agility-focused one — but consider your Profession's core skills when distributing points.

## Step Three: Skills

You have 15 skill points to distribute among skills. Each skill is linked to an Attribute. Raising a skill to its linked Attribute's die type or below costs 1 point per step. Raising it above the linked Attribute costs 2 points per step.

**Example:** If your Agility is d8 and you want Fighting d10, the first four raises (d4 to d8) cost 1 point each. The raise from d8 to d10 costs 2 points.

### Core Skills

Every character begins with the following skills at d4 at no cost. These represent the baseline competence of a person living in Nippon:

- **Athletics** (Agility)
- **Common Knowledge** (Smarts)
- **Notice** (Smarts)
- **Persuasion** (Spirit) — *Note: In Savage Bushido, Socialize replaces Persuasion for most social interactions. Persuasion still exists for direct emotional appeals.*
- **Stealth** (Agility)

### Profession Starting Skills

Your Profession grants additional free skill raises. These are listed in Chapter Four under each Profession's entry. Apply these after distributing your 15 points.

### Nippon Skills

The full skill list for Savage Bushido differs from standard SWADE in several places. Key differences:

- **Socialize** replaces much of what Persuasion does in standard SWADE — it covers formal social interaction, gift-giving protocol, and reading social situations
- **Warfare** replaces Knowledge (Battle/Strategy) — it covers tactical command, siege warfare, and Mass Battle
- **Perform** covers all artistic performance — music, poetry, dance, tea ceremony as performance
- **Craft** covers all skilled making — swords, armor, pottery, calligraphy
- **Butsudo, Shinten, and Shugendo** are the three arcane skills, available only to characters with the appropriate Arcane Background

The complete skill list with descriptions appears in Chapter Three.

## Step Four: Edges and Hindrances

### Hindrances

You may take up to one Major Hindrance and two Minor Hindrances. Each gives you points to spend on additional advances:

- **Major Hindrance:** 2 points
- **Minor Hindrance:** 1 point each

Spend Hindrance points as follows:
- 2 points: raise an Attribute one die type
- 2 points: gain an Edge
- 1 point: gain 5 skill points
- 1 point: gain 500 additional gold pieces (expressed in Ryo)

### Edges

You begin with one free Edge. Choose it from the full Edge list in Chapter Five, subject to your Rank (Novice) and any Profession requirements.

**Bugei Edges from Birth and Profession are free** — they do not come from this Edge selection. Your starting free Edge should be something additional: a Background Edge, a Social Edge, a Leadership Edge, or a second Bugei Category Omote if your GM allows.

### Important: Bugei Starting Edges

In addition to your free starting Edge, you receive Bugei Edges from two sources:

**From Birth (Step Zero):** One Category Omote Edge (the broad martial family your birth background trained you in). High Buke receive two.

**From Profession (Step One):** One Tier 2 Specialist Edge in your Profession's primary weapon tradition. This represents your formal training. A Bushi receives Kenjutsu (the Katana specialist Edge). A Budoka receives Atemi-waza. A Sohei receives Naginatajutsu or Bojutsu. Details in Chapter Four.

These Bugei Edges are recorded separately from your standard Edges on your character sheet, in the Bugei section.

## Step Five: Derived Statistics

Calculate the following from your Attributes and Edges:

| Statistic | Calculation |
|---|---|
| Pace | 6 (modified by Edges and Hindrances) |
| Parry | 2 + half Fighting die type (round down). Weapon bonuses add to this. |
| Toughness | 2 + half Vigor die type (round down). Armor adds to this. |
| On | Set in Step Zero (Birth). Modified by Profession in Step One. |

**Example:** A character with Fighting d8 has Parry 6. With a Katana (Parry +1 for the Kenjutsu Edge), Parry becomes 7.

## Step Six: On Score

Your On score was set at Birth. Your Profession may modify it:

| Profession | On Modifier |
|---|---|
| Bushi | +0 |
| Budoka | −1 (martial artists are respected but not high-status) |
| Gakusho (Buddhist) | +2 (clergy carry social weight) |
| Gakusho (Shinto) | +2 |
| Sohei | +1 |
| Shugenja | −2 (mountain sorcerers are feared and marginal) |
| Yakuza | −3 (criminals have notoriously low On) |

Record your final starting On. This number changes constantly in play — see Chapter Seven for the full On system.

### On and Social Rolls

Your On modifier applies to social skill rolls when the target knows your standing:

| On Score | Social Modifier |
|---|---|
| 1–4 | −2 |
| 5–7 | −1 |
| 8–12 | +0 |
| 13–16 | +1 |
| 17–20 | +2 |

## Step Seven: Gear and Starting Funds

### Starting Equipment by Birth

**High Buke:** Katana and Wakizashi (the Dai-sho — worn as a pair, the mark of Samurai status), Light Samurai armor, one warhorse (chance equal to starting On as a percentage), formal court clothes, traveling clothes.

**Middle Buke:** Katana and Wakizashi, Light Samurai armor, traveling clothes. Chance of a horse.

**Low Buke:** Wakizashi (may not own a Katana until they earn it through service or purchase), Light Ashigaru armor, traveling clothes.

**Ronin by Birth:** Katana or Wakizashi (one only, GM's choice or player rolls), no armor, traveling clothes.

**Merchant:** No weapons (a knife, legally), good traveling clothes, a strongbox, 2d6 × 5 Ryo in starting capital.

**Artisan:** No weapons, working clothes, tools of their trade, 1d6 × 5 Ryo.

**Farmer:** Bo staff or farm tool (not a weapon legally, but functional), rough clothes, 1d6 × 2 Ryo.

### Currency

Nippon uses three currency denominations:

| Coin | Material | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Ryo | Gold | 1 Ryo = 10 Shu |
| Shu | Silver | 1 Shu = 10 Mon |
| Mon | Copper | Base unit |

A common retainer Samurai earns 1–3 Ryo per month as stipend. A skilled craftsman earns 5–10 Shu per day. A laborer earns 1–5 Mon per day.

### Weapons and Armor

Full weapon and armor tables appear in Chapter Six. Key starting weapons:

| Weapon | Damage | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Katana | Str+d6, AP 1 | Parry +1 with Kenjutsu Edge |
| Wakizashi | Str+d6 | The short sword of the Dai-sho |
| Tanto | Str+d4 | Dagger; easily concealed |
| Yari | Str+d6, Reach 1 | The standard spear |
| Bo | Str+d4, Parry +1, Reach 1 | Staff; Sohei and Budoka weapon |
| Naginata | Str+d6+1, Reach 1 | Glaive; Sohei primary weapon |

| Armor | Armor Bonus | Min Strength | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Light Ashigaru | +2 | d6 | Basic infantry armor |
| Heavy Ashigaru | +3 | d8 | Full infantry kit |
| Light Samurai | +2 | d6 | Social bonus in formal contexts |
| Heavy Samurai | +4 | d10 | Full battle armor |

## Step Eight: Arcane Backgrounds

If your Profession is Gakusho (Buddhist), Gakusho (Shinto), Sohei, or Shugenja, you have an Arcane Background. Record the following:

### Shugenja — Shugendo

- **Arcane Skill:** Shugendo (Smarts)
- **Starting Power Points:** 10
- **Starting Powers:** 3, chosen from your School's power list (see Appendix C)
- **School:** Choose one at character creation: Hi-do (Fire), Mizu-do (Water), Hayashi-do (Wood), Kane-do (Metal), or Tsuchi-do (Soil). This choice is permanent.

### Gakusho (Buddhist) — Buddhist Miracles

- **Arcane Skill:** Butsudo (Spirit)
- **Starting Power Points:** 10
- **Starting Powers:** 3, chosen from the Buddhist power list (see Appendix C)
- **Pollution:** Begins at 0. Tracked separately from standard game mechanics.
- **Sacred Texts:** Begin with access to two Sacred Texts from the Buddhist list.

### Gakusho (Shinto) — Shinto Miracles

- **Arcane Skill:** Shinten (Spirit)
- **Starting Power Points:** 10
- **Starting Powers:** 3, chosen from the Shinto power list (see Appendix C)
- **Kegare:** Begins at 0. The Shinto equivalent of Pollution.
- **Sacred Texts:** Begin with access to two Sacred Texts from the Shinto list.

### Sohei — Buddhist Miracles (limited)

- **Arcane Skill:** Butsudo (Spirit)
- **Starting Power Points:** 10
- **Starting Powers:** 2, chosen from the Buddhist power list
- **Pollution:** Begins at 0. Sohei accumulate Pollution more slowly than Gakusho when engaged in righteous defense.

## Step Nine: Background and Connections

### Name

Choose a name appropriate to your Birth and Profession. Buke characters have a clan name followed by a personal name — Ryojii Korisawa, Tamekura Borukuro. Heimin typically use a single personal name. Gakusho who have taken vows use a dharma name. Appendix E contains full name tables for random generation or deliberate selection.

### Profession Chain Position

Note where you stand in your Profession's advancement chain. All characters begin at Novice rank. Chapter Four shows the full chain for each Profession — you'll want to know where you're headed before play begins.

### Group Rank

If you are joining an organization at the start of play (a clan, a temple, a Ryu, a criminal gang), record your starting Group Rank. New members typically begin at Group Rank 0 or 1 depending on circumstance. The full Group Rank system appears in Chapter Seven.

### Obligations and Connections

Work with your GM to establish:

- **Who do you serve?** Most Buke are retainers to a lord. Most Gakusho belong to a temple or shrine. Most Yakuza have an Oyabun. Who is above you in the hierarchy, and what do they expect of you?
- **Who do you owe?** Obligation is currency in Nippon. Starting debts of honor — a teacher who trained you, a patron who sponsored you, a rival you humiliated — create immediate story.
- **Who owes you?** Even a Novice character has done something. What is it, and who remembers?

| Zero: Birth | Roll or choose. Determines On, class, scripts, and one free Bugei Category Omote. |
