Edges are the primary advancement currency of Savage Bushido. Every advance you earn can become an Edge — a new capability, a deepened skill, a mastered technique, or a social advantage. The Edge list is long because Nippon rewards specialization. A Bushi who has spent three advances on the Zanshin chain fights very differently from one who has spent those same advances on social and leadership Edges.

This chapter organizes Edges into categories. Read through the categories relevant to your Profession before play begins — knowing what's available shapes how you develop your character.

## Edge Requirements

Edges list their requirements in order:
1. Wild Card or not (most Edges require Wild Card status)
2. Rank (Novice, Seasoned, Veteran, Heroic, Legendary)
3. Attribute minimums
4. Prior Edges required
5. Skill minimums
6. Other conditions (Profession, teacher, etc.)

All player characters are Wild Cards. For NPCs, the GM determines Wild Card status.

## Background Edges

Background Edges represent advantages of birth, circumstance, or early training that don't fit neatly into other categories. They are typically taken at character creation but may be taken later if the GM agrees the character has earned the background through play.

### Buke Blood
*Novice*

You are unambiguously of the warrior class — your bearing, your manners, and your sword mark you as Samurai even without introduction. +1 On in any situation where your Buke status is relevant. Heimin characters may not take this Edge.

### Merchant's Eye
*Novice, Smarts d6+*

A lifetime among commerce has given you an instinct for value and deception in transactions. +2 to Notice rolls to detect fraudulent goods, weighted dice, or dishonest dealing. +1 to all Commerce-related Knowledge rolls.

### Well Connected
*Novice, Socialize d6+*

You have cultivated a broad network of acquaintances across social boundaries. Once per session, you may call on a contact for information or minor assistance without a Streetwise or Socialize roll.

### Dojo Trained
*Novice, Fighting d6+*

You received formal training at a recognized Ryu rather than informal instruction. +1 to all rolls made to impress or be recognized by other martial artists. When seeking a teacher for Okuden, the TN of Socialize rolls to find one is reduced by 1.

### Former Life
*Novice*

You left a previous Profession behind — a warrior who became a monk, a priest who returned to the world, a criminal who went straight. You retain one starting skill from your former Profession at d4 (free). Work with your GM to establish the circumstances.

### Heir
*Novice, On 8+*

You are the recognized heir of your family or clan — the next in line for the family's holdings, responsibilities, and obligations. You have not yet inherited, but everyone knows you will. +1 to all Socialize rolls with members of your clan or allied clans. The GM may introduce complications appropriate to being heir — rivals, obligations, expectations.

## Combat Edges

Combat Edges improve fighting capability through trained technique. Most are available to any character of sufficient rank and skill — they are not Profession-locked.

### Block
*Seasoned, Fighting d8+*

+1 Parry. May be taken twice (Improved Block, Veteran, Fighting d10+): +2 Parry total.

### Combat Reflexes
*Seasoned*

+2 to recover from Shaken in combat.

### Counterattack
*Seasoned, Fighting d8+*

Once per round, when an adjacent foe misses with a melee attack, you may immediately make a free Fighting roll against them at −2. The free attack does not count toward multi-action limits. May be taken twice (Improved Counterattack, Veteran, Fighting d10+): the −2 penalty is removed.

### First Strike
*Novice, Agility d6+*

Once per round, when a foe moves adjacent to you, you may make a free Fighting roll against them before they act. May be taken twice (Improved First Strike, Heroic, Agility d8+): may use First Strike against every foe who moves adjacent in a round.

### Frenzy
*Seasoned, Fighting d8+*

May make two Fighting attacks in one action at −2 each. May be taken twice (Improved Frenzy, Veteran, Fighting d10+): two attacks at no penalty.

### Hard to Kill
*Novice, Spirit d8+*

+2 to Vigor rolls made to avoid death when Incapacitated.

### Level Headed
*Seasoned, Smarts d8+*

Draw two Action Cards at the start of each round and act on the higher. May be taken twice (Improved Level Headed, Heroic): draw three cards, act on the highest.

### Nerves of Steel
*Novice, Vigor d8+*

May ignore 1 point of Wound penalty. May be taken twice (Improved Nerves of Steel, Seasoned, Vigor d10+): ignore 2 points of Wound penalty.

### Quick
*Novice, Agility d8+*

If dealt a Five or lower for an Action Card, discard and draw again until you receive higher than a Five.

### Sweep
*Novice, Strength d6+, Fighting d8+*

May attack all adjacent foes in one action at −2. All targets use the same roll result.

### Two-Fisted
*Novice, Agility d8+*

May attack with a weapon in each hand with no off-hand penalty. Both attacks are made simultaneously. Must still account for multi-action penalties for additional actions beyond the two attacks.

### Whirlwind Cut
*Heroic, Fighting d10+, Sweep*

As Sweep but with no penalty. May be combined with Frenzy for two full Sweep attacks at no penalty.

## Bugei Edges — Tier 1: Category Omote

Category Omote Edges represent broad martial families — the general tradition your birth or early training placed you in. They unlock Tier 2 Specialist Edges and provide a baseline competence across a weapon family.

### Edged Weapons
*Novice, Fighting d6+*

Training in all edged weapons — Katana, Wakizashi, Tanto, Naginata, and similar blades. +1 to Fighting rolls when using edged weapons. Unlocks all Edged Weapons Specialist Edges.

### Pole Weapons
*Novice, Fighting d6+*

Training in all pole weapons — Yari, Naginata, Bo, and similar long weapons. +1 to Fighting rolls when using pole weapons. Reach 1 weapons grant Parry +1 while held. Unlocks all Pole Weapons Specialist Edges.

### Unarmed Combat
*Novice, Fighting d6+*

Training in unarmed fighting systems — striking, grappling, throwing. +1 to Fighting rolls when unarmed. Never considered unarmed (no −2 penalty). Parry never reduced for fighting without a weapon. Unlocks all Unarmed Combat Specialist Edges.

### Missile Weapons
*Novice, Shooting d6+*

Training in all ranged weapons — bows, thrown weapons, and firearms. +1 to Shooting rolls. Unlocks all Missile Weapons Specialist Edges.

### Peasant Weapons
*Novice, Fighting d6+, Budoka or Yakuza Profession OR Heimin birth*

Training in improvised and working-class weapons — chain weapons, agricultural tools, tonfa, sai, nunchaku. +1 to Fighting rolls with these weapons. Unlocks all Peasant Weapons Specialist Edges.

## Bugei Edges — Tier 2: Specialist

Specialist Edges provide mastery of a specific weapon or fighting style within a broad category. Each requires the appropriate Category Omote Edge.

### Kenjutsu
*Novice, Edged Weapons, Fighting d6+*

Mastery of the katana. Parry +1 when using a katana. A raise on a Fighting roll with a katana upgrades the damage die (d6 → d8 → d10 → d12). Unlocks Iai-jutsu and Nito-kenjutsu Okuden.

### Tantojutsu
*Novice, Edged Weapons, Fighting d6+*

Mastery of the knife and short blade. Tanto attacks ignore 2 points of Armor (AP 2 total). Concealable: Notice rolls to detect a concealed tanto are at −2.

### Kamajutsu
*Novice, Edged Weapons OR Peasant Weapons, Fighting d6+*

Mastery of the kama (sickle) and kusari-gama (chain sickle). On a raise, the opponent is Entangled (Fighting vs. Athletics to escape each round).

### Naginatajutsu
*Novice, Pole Weapons, Fighting d6+*

Mastery of the naginata (glaive). Naginata damage becomes Str+d6+1. Reach 1. +1 damage against mounted opponents. Unlocks Naginata Okuden.

### Sojutsu
*Novice, Pole Weapons, Fighting d6+*

Mastery of the yari (spear). A raise on a Fighting roll with a yari pushes the target back 1" and may knock them Prone (Agility roll TN 4 to avoid). Unlocks Sojutsu Okuden.

### Bojutsu
*Novice, Pole Weapons, Fighting d6+*

Mastery of the bo staff. Bo attacks deal Str+d4 but grant Parry +2 (total Parry +3 with Pole Weapons Omote). May use Sweep with a bo without the Sweep Edge.

### Atemi-waza
*Novice, Unarmed Combat, Fighting d6+*

Unarmed attacks deal Str+d6 (upgraded from default Str+d4). +2 to Grapple rolls. Unlocks Dim Mak Okuden.

### Sumaijutsu
*Novice, Unarmed Combat, Fighting d6+*

Mastery of wrestling and throwing. On a raise, may choose to throw the opponent Prone (no damage) instead of dealing damage. Thrown opponents take Str+d4 damage from the fall and are Prone.

### Jujutsu
*Seasoned, Unarmed Combat, Fighting d8+, Atemi-waza OR Sumaijutsu*

Combined striking and grappling system. When an opponent misses with a melee attack, may make an immediate free Grapple attempt against them. On a successful Grapple, may choose to throw (Prone + damage) or lock (Entangled, Fighting vs. Athletics to escape).

### Kyujutsu
*Novice, Missile Weapons, Shooting d6+*

Mastery of the bow. May fire a bow as a semi-automatic weapon (ROF 2 at −2). A raise on a Shooting roll adds AP 2 to that shot (the arrow finds a gap in armor). Unlocks Yabusame Okuden.

### Teppojutsu
*Novice, Missile Weapons, Shooting d6+*

Mastery of the tanegashima arquebus. Reduces reload time to one action (standard is two). +2 to Shooting rolls when firing from a prepared, stationary position.

### Jittejutsu
*Novice, Peasant Weapons, Fighting d6+*

Mastery of the jitte (police truncheon). +2 to Disarm attempts. On a raise on a Disarm, the weapon lands 1d6" away in a direction of your choice. Subdual damage by default.

### Tessenjutsu
*Novice, Peasant Weapons OR Edged Weapons, Fighting d6+*

Mastery of the iron war fan. Tessen attacks deal Str+d4. When used defensively, +1 Parry. May be used to deflect one ranged attack per round (Fighting roll TN equal to the attacker's Shooting result).

### Hojojutsu
*Seasoned, Peasant Weapons, Fighting d8+*

Mastery of rope-binding — the art of capturing and restraining opponents. A successful Grapple with rope or chain Entangles the opponent. With a raise, the target is Bound (cannot move or take physical actions). Bound condition requires a raise on a Strength roll to escape.

### Nunchaku-te
*Novice, Peasant Weapons, Fighting d6+*

Mastery of nunchaku. Attacks deal Str+d4. On a raise, the target is Distracted until end of next turn (the weapon wraps unpredictably). +1 to disarm attempts.

### Sai-te
*Novice, Peasant Weapons, Fighting d6+*

Mastery of the sai (three-pronged truncheon). +2 to Parry against edged weapons. May perform a Disarm as a free action when an opponent scores a non-raise melee hit against you.

### Tonfa-te
*Novice, Peasant Weapons, Fighting d6+*

Mastery of the tonfa (side-handle baton). +1 Parry. Attacks deal Str+d4. May make two attacks per action with no multi-action penalty (the tonfa's blocking face covers the off-hand automatically).

## Bugei Edges — Tier 3: Okuden

Okuden are secret techniques available only to those who have found and satisfied a qualified teacher. They cannot be taken at character creation. They cannot be purchased with Advances unless the teacher relationship has been established in play.

### Iai-jutsu
*Seasoned, Kenjutsu, Fighting d8+, teacher required*

Draw and execute a full-damage attack as a single action (normally drawing is a free action but the attack is a separate action). When beginning a Formal Duel in the sheathed-start form, Iai-jutsu practitioners act before all other combatants in Round 1, regardless of Action Card. +2 to damage against targets who are unaware of the attack.

### Nito-kenjutsu
*Veteran, Kenjutsu, Fighting d10+, teacher required*

Wield a katana and wakizashi simultaneously. The off-hand wakizashi grants Parry +1. May make a second attack with the wakizashi at −2 (this is a separate action subject to multi-action rules). The combination is visually distinctive — opponents know they face a dual-sword practitioner.

### Yadomajutsu
*Seasoned, Kenjutsu OR Kyujutsu, Fighting OR Shooting d8+, teacher required*

Once per round, may attempt to deflect one incoming ranged attack. Make a Fighting roll against the attacker's Shooting result. With success, the projectile is deflected harmlessly. With a raise, the projectile is deflected toward a target of your choice within range.

### Improved Yadomajutsu
*Veteran, Yadomajutsu, Fighting d10+*

May attempt to deflect all incoming ranged attacks in a round, one at a time, until you fail a deflection roll.

### Yabusame
*Veteran, Kyujutsu, Riding d8+, teacher required*

No penalty for shooting from horseback. Additionally, when firing from horseback at a gallop, gain +1 to Shooting rolls (the momentum adds power and the height adds angle).

### Piercing Thrust
*Seasoned, Sojutsu, Fighting d8+, teacher required*

A full-commitment spear thrust that drives through armor. The attack deals full damage. On a Shaken result (not a Wound), the target is pushed back 1" and may fall Prone (Agility roll TN 4).

### Iron Wall
*Veteran, Sojutsu, Fighting d10+, teacher required*

Adopt a defensive spear stance as a free action. While in Iron Wall stance: Parry +2 against charges, +4 damage against any target who moves into your Reach to attack you. You may not move while in Iron Wall stance.

### Dim Mak
*See Budoka Advancement Chain*

The Dim Mak Okuden (Novice, Veteran, Heroic) are detailed under the Budoka Profession chain in Chapter Four.

### Kusari-jutsu
*Seasoned, Peasant Weapons, Fighting d8+, Kamajutsu OR Hojojutsu, teacher required*

Mastery of weighted chain as a primary weapon. Chain weapons (kusari-gama, manriki-gusari) gain: Reach 2 (the chain extends), AP 1 (the weight bypasses light armor), and on a raise the target is Entangled. The chain may be used to Disarm at Reach 2 with +2 to the attempt.

## Yoga Scholar Edges

The Five Yogas are the elemental Schools of Shugendo — Hi-do, Mizu-do, Hayashi-do, Kane-do, and Tsuchi-do — as understood through a religious lens. A Gakusho who masters a Yoga petitions the elemental forces through faith, receiving them as expressions of divine will rather than raw natural power.

### Yoga Scholar (Buddhist)
*Veteran, Gakusho (Buddhist), Dharma Master, Knowledge (Buddhism) d8+*

The Buddhist scholar has studied the Five Yogas through esoteric Buddhist teaching. Gain access to one Shugendo School's Novice and Seasoned rank powers, cast using Butsudo.

**School Selection:** Choose one School permanently: Hi-do, Mizu-do, Hayashi-do, Kane-do, or Tsuchi-do.

**Power Access:** May learn any Novice or Seasoned rank powers from the chosen School. Cast with Butsudo, drawing from the same PP pool as other powers.

**Religious Trapping:** All powers carry Buddhist trappings — fire is the purifying wisdom-flame, water is the flow of compassion, earth is the groundedness of meditation.

**The Ceiling:** May never access Veteran, Heroic, or Legendary Shugendo powers through this Edge. Those require the Shugenja's dedicated elemental training.

**Pollution:** Powers cast through Yoga Scholar still accumulate Pollution under standard Buddhist rules.

### Yoga Scholar (Shinto)
*Veteran, Gakusho (Shinto), Purification Rite, Knowledge (Shinto) d8+*

The Shinto practitioner has cultivated a relationship with the elemental Kami, who grant elemental power through faith. Access to one Shugendo School's Novice and Seasoned rank powers, cast using Shinten.

**School Selection, Power Access, The Ceiling:** As Yoga Scholar (Buddhist).

**Kami Trapping:** All powers carry Kami/Shinto trappings — fire comes from a fire Kami's blessing, earth holds because the earth Kami wills it. Each casting is a petition, not a command.

**Kegare:** Powers cast through Yoga Scholar accumulate Kegare under standard Shinto rules.

### Five Schools — Power Reference

| School | Element | Key Novice/Seasoned Powers |
|---|---|---|
| Hi-do | Fire | *bolt* (fire), *protection* (fire shield), *smite* (fire touch), *blast* (fire), *barrier* (wall of flame) |
| Mizu-do | Water | *bolt* (water force), *entangle* (water/ice), *illusion* (reflection), *environmental protection* (water breathing), *barrier* (water walls) |
| Hayashi-do | Wood | *bolt* (thorn), *darksight* (shadow eyes), *detect/conceal arcana*, *beast friend*, *wall walker*, *farsight* |
| Kane-do | Metal | *smite* (animated blade), *armor* (metal scales), *bolt* (metal AP), *confusion* (glinting gaze), *deflection*, *puppet* (conquering gaze) |
| Tsuchi-do | Soil | *entangle* (traitor ground), *healing* (pure flesh), *relief*, *environmental protection*, *bolt* (earth/stone), *arcane protection*, *barrier* (stone walls) |

## Social Edges

Social Edges improve a character's effectiveness in the formal social world of Nippon — court interactions, organizational politics, and the gift economy.

### Charismatic
*Novice, Socialize d6+*

+2 to all Socialize rolls. This is the standard SWADE Charismatic Edge, fully applicable in Nippon.

### Commanding Presence
*Seasoned, Spirit d8+, Socialize d6+*

When you enter a formal social situation, all present immediately know you are someone significant, regardless of whether they know your name. NPCs begin interactions at Neutral rather than their default Disposition toward strangers (which is often Uncooperative).

### Connections
*Novice*

Has a specific organizational connection — a relative in a powerful clan, a temple patron, a mentor in the Shogunate. Once per session, may call on this connection for information, introductions, or minor material assistance. The connection has its own interests and may ask for things in return.

### Gift Giver
*Novice, Socialize d6+*

Understanding the art of giving appropriate gifts is a social skill in itself. +2 to Socialize rolls when a gift is part of the social interaction. With a raise on the Socialize roll, the gift creates an obligation bond — the recipient owes a favor.

### Silver Tongue
*Novice, Smarts d8+*

+2 to Taunt rolls. May use Taunt as a social action in Tier 3 Social Conflict without automatically creating hostility (normally, using Taunt in formal settings costs On).

## Leadership Edges

Leadership Edges are relevant for characters who command others — whether military units, criminal gangs, or religious institutions.

### Command
*Novice, Smarts d6+*

Allies within Command Range (5") who can see and hear you may use your Smarts die instead of their own when recovering from Shaken.

### Hold the Line
*Seasoned, Command, Smarts d8+*

Allies within Command Range gain +1 Toughness.

### Inspire
*Seasoned, Command*

Once per combat, may give an inspiring speech (free action, one round). All allies within Command Range gain +2 to their next trait roll.

### Natural Leader
*Novice, Spirit d8+*

Allies within Command Range gain one additional Benny at the start of each session.

### Tactician
*Seasoned, Smarts d8+, Warfare d6+*

Once per combat, may draw an additional Action Card and give it to any ally within Command Range.

### Warlord
*Heroic, Command, Warfare d8+*

+2 to Battle Rolls in Mass Battle. Allies within Command Range gain +1 to Fighting rolls.

## Arcane Edges

Arcane Edges are available to characters with an Arcane Background. They improve arcane capability, PP management, and specialized arcane techniques.

### New Powers
*Novice, Arcane Background*

Gain two new powers from the appropriate tradition's power list.

### Power Points
*Novice, Arcane Background*

Gain 5 additional Power Points. May be taken multiple times.

### Rapid Recharge
*Seasoned, Arcane Background, Spirit d6+*

Recover 1 PP per 30 minutes of rest (standard is 1 per hour).

### Improved Rapid Recharge
*Veteran, Rapid Recharge, Spirit d8+*

Recover 1 PP per 15 minutes of rest.

### Channeling
*Seasoned, Arcane Background*

Once per session, cast one power without spending PP.

### Arcane Resistance
*Novice*

−2 to arcane skill rolls targeting this character. −2 to magical damage against this character. May be taken twice (Improved Arcane Resistance): −4 to both.

### Onmyoji Initiate
*Novice, Knowledge (Divination) d6+, Smarts d8+*

Access to Arcane Background (Onmyoji) — the court art of divination and geomancy practiced by court specialists. Arcane skill: Divination (Smarts). 3 starting powers: *detect/conceal arcana*, *locate*, *farsight*. 10 PP. Restricted to Kuge or Gakusho background, or those sponsored by a court Onmyoji.

## Vocational Edges

Vocational Edges represent professional specializations that don't fit combat or social categories.

### Healer
*Novice, Healing d6+*

+2 to Healing rolls. A raise on a Healing roll removes two Wounds.

### Scholar
*Novice, Smarts d8+*

+2 to all Knowledge skill rolls. The breadth of learning complements depth.

### Survivalist
*Novice, Survival d8+*

+2 to Survival rolls. May find food and water for a group of up to six without a roll in most wilderness environments.

### Streetwise (Edge)
*Novice, Streetwise d6+*

+2 to Streetwise rolls. Contacts in the criminal underworld are available even in unfamiliar cities at TN 6.

### Master Craftsman
*Veteran, Craft d10+*

+2 to Craft rolls. May attempt Master-quality work (normally requiring three raises) with only two raises.

## Supernatural Edges

Supernatural Edges represent abilities that touch the spirit world without being formal arcane powers.

### Kami Sense
*Novice, Spirit d6+, Gakusho (Shinto) OR Shugenja*

Passive awareness of supernatural presences within Smarts" at all times. No roll required. Already listed in relevant Profession chains — this Edge allows other characters to develop the ability.

### Spirit Sight
*Seasoned, Spirit d8+*

May see spirits and supernatural beings even when they are invisible or in spirit form, with a Notice roll (TN 4).

### Lucky
*Novice*

One additional Benny per session.

### Very Lucky
*Seasoned, Lucky*

Two additional Bennies per session.

## Legendary Edges

Legendary Edges are available only at Heroic and Legendary rank. They represent capabilities beyond normal human development.

### Legendary Warrior
*Heroic, Fighting d12+*

+1 to all Fighting rolls. A raise on any melee attack allows an immediate free attack against the same target (this free attack cannot itself generate another free attack).

### Living Legend
*Legendary, On 16+*

Your reputation is complete. All enemies who know your name must make a Spirit roll (TN 4) before willingly engaging you in direct confrontation. Failure means they will attempt to find another way — ambush, poison, political action — before facing you openly.

### Master of War
*Heroic, Warfare d10+, Tactician*

+4 to Battle Rolls in Mass Battle. May reroll one Battle Roll per Mass Battle encounter (must keep the second result).

## Hindrances

Hindrances represent significant personal limitations — physical, psychological, or social — that create ongoing complications in play. In exchange for accepting these limitations, you gain Hindrance points at character creation (see Chapter Two).

Hindrances in Savage Bushido function as standard SWADE Hindrances with the following additions specific to the setting:

### Code of Honor (Major)
You hold yourself to a strict personal code — the specific content is defined by your Profession and background, but the effect is the same: you will not violate the code even when violation would be tactically advantageous. Specify the code's key prohibitions at character creation. Violations cost 1d4 On immediately plus whatever the GM determines appropriate to the situation.

### Masterless (Major)
You are a Ronin — a Samurai without a lord. −2 On in social settings where status matters. Cannot join most formal organizations without first proving yourself. On the other hand, your obligations are your own.

### Obligation — Minor or Major
A specific, named obligation that creates regular demands on your time, resources, or loyalty. Minor: the obligation demands attention occasionally and creates minor complications. Major: the obligation is constant and may override your own interests.

### Outsider (Minor or Major)
You are not from here — foreign-born, from a distant province, or of the Ainu people of the northern islands. Minor: unfamiliar customs cause occasional friction. Major: you face active prejudice that closes social doors.

### Pacifist (Major)
You will not kill. This is most common for Buddhist Gakusho and some Sohei. You may fight in subdual (see Chapter Six) but will not deliver a killing blow, even to Oni or Yurei. You must find other solutions.

### Poverty (Minor)
You begin with half the starting funds appropriate to your Birth, and money tends to flow away from you. Good for characters whose Profession involves vows of poverty (Gakusho) or who have lost their family holdings (some Ronin).

### Vow (Minor or Major)
A specific oath that constrains your behavior. Minor: the vow affects a narrow range of situations. Major: the vow shapes major life decisions. Gakusho who have taken formal religious vows typically take this Hindrance to reflect their religious obligations.
